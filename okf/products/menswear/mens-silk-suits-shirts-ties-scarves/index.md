Most “Silk” Sold Online Is Polyester With a Shiny Finish. Ours Is Real.
Search for a silk suit or a silk shirt and you will find hundreds of them between $20 and $80. Almost none of them are silk. They are polyester woven in a satin weave to mimic the sheen, and they give the game away the moment you wear them: they trap heat, build static, and read synthetic under any real light. Real silk does the opposite. It carries a soft, natural luster rather than a plastic shine, it breathes and regulates temperature because it is a protein fiber, and it drapes in a way no synthetic can copy.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Is this real silk or polyester?  
**A:** Every garment here is genuine silk, never polyester. The suits and trousers are 100% dupioni silk, the blazers and shirts come in raw silk and 100% satin silk, the Nehru jacket is noil silk, and the ties, pocket squares, and scarves are 100% satin silk. Most items sold online as &#8220;silk&#8221; for $20 to $80 are polyester woven in a satin weave to imitate the sheen. Ours are natural silk fiber, which is why they carry a soft luster rather than a plastic shine, breathe against the skin, and drape the way silk should.

**Q:** What is the difference between dupioni, raw, and satin silk?  
**A:** Dupioni silk is crisp with a subtle, irregular slub texture and a soft luster; it holds structure well, which makes it ideal for suits and trousers. Raw silk is woven from shorter fibers and has a characterful, nubby, low-luster surface with a relaxed, textural feel, best in blazers and casual shirts. Satin silk is woven in a satin weave that produces a smooth, lustrous face, which is why it is used for the shirts, ties, pocket squares, and scarves. All three are natural silk; they simply differ in weave and character.

**Q:** Is a silk suit a good choice for a wedding?  
**A:** Yes. Dupioni silk is one of the most popular fabrics for formal menswear because its natural texture and soft luster photograph beautifully both indoors and outdoors, which matters enormously in wedding images. Black, ivory, navy, and burgundy are the most common wedding choices among our customers. Because each suit is made to your measurements with a free test suit first, the fit is confirmed before we cut the silk, so a groom or guest gets a photograph-ready suit with no sizing risk.

**Q:** Does silk keep you cool or warm?  
**A:** Both, because natural silk is a protein fiber that regulates temperature. It breathes and feels cool in warm weather, yet it insulates and feels warm against the skin in the cold, which is exactly why a silk shirt or scarf is comfortable across seasons. Polyester satin does neither; it traps heat and builds static. This temperature regulation is one of the clearest practical differences between real silk and the synthetic imitation.

**Q:** Can I wear a silk shirt formally or casually?  
**A:** Both, depending on color and how you style it. In off-white, charcoal, navy, or black, a satin silk shirt worn under a suit or tuxedo reads formal and refined, and pairs cleanly with a Baron tuxedo. In blood red, royal purple, or sage green worn with dark trousers and no tie, the same shirt becomes a statement piece for an evening event. Because it is cut to your measurements, it looks intentional in either register rather than dressed up from something casual.

**Q:** How do I care for silk?  
**A:** The suits, blazers, shirts, trousers, and Nehru jacket are dry clean only, which protects the fiber, the color, and the drape. The satin silk accessories can be dry cleaned or gently hand washed in cold water with a mild silk-friendly detergent, then air dried in the shade. Iron silk on low heat, ideally on the reverse side or with a pressing cloth, and never with high direct heat. Cared for correctly, natural silk keeps its luster and hand for years.

**Q:** Does silk wrinkle easily?  
**A:** Silk relaxes rather than holding hard creases, and the woven silks here, dupioni and raw silk, are more forgiving than fine charmeuse. Dupioni&#8217;s slub texture actually hides light creasing well, and the satin silk shirt is described as requiring minimal ironing. Any light wrinkling releases quickly with a low-heat iron on the reverse or a gentle steam. Hanging garments on shaped hangers between wears keeps them looking their best.
