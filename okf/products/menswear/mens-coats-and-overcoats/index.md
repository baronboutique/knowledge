## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Are these coats made to measure or off the rack?  
**A:** Every coat is made to your measurements — there is no off-the-rack sizing. You submit your chest, shoulder, and sleeve measurements at checkout. We construct a free cotton test coat first, ship it to you to check the fit, and only cut the final coat once you confirm it is right. Nothing is finalised until the fit is confirmed.

**Q:** What is the difference between a topcoat, overcoat, and peacoat?  
**A:** A topcoat is lightweight, single-breasted, and typically falls above the knee, designed for transitional weather and layering over a suit without adding bulk. An overcoat is heavier and longer, usually knee-length or below, built for colder weather and formal layering. A peacoat is double-breasted, mid-thigh in length, with wide lapels; naval in origin, now versatile across smart-casual and formal contexts. All three styles are available in this collection.

**Q:** What fabric is best for a men&#8217;s wool overcoat?  
**A:** For a classic overcoat worn over a suit, English Melton wool is the strongest choice — dense, structured, and naturally insulating without being heavy. For a softer handle and added warmth, a wool-cashmere blend is the step up. Super 120s flannel sits between the two, lighter than Melton, with a finer finish suited to formal city wear. Each product page specifies the exact cloth used and its weight.

**Q:** How should a men&#8217;s overcoat fit when worn over a suit jacket?  
**A:** The shoulders of the overcoat should align cleanly with your suit shoulders, no pulling or overhang. The sleeve should be long enough to cover your shirt cuff but reveal a sliver of your suit sleeve below it. Through the chest, there should be enough room to button the overcoat comfortably with a jacket underneath. Because all coats here are built to your measurements, the fit over a jacket is factored into the construction from the start.

**Q:** Do you make screen-accurate movie and TV replica coats?  
**A:** Yes — replica coats are a significant part of this collection. Available replicas include multiple Doctor Who coats (12th Doctor Series 9 and 10, velvet variants in black, navy, and maroon), James Bond coats from Spectre and Quantum of Solace, Sherlock Holmes, The Matrix Revolutions, Fantastic Beasts, and others. Each replica is researched for accuracy in fabric type, lapel shape, button placement, and silhouette.

**Q:** What coat length should I choose?  
**A:** Car coats sitting at the hip are the most practical for everyday use and work well on shorter frames. Mid-thigh length is the most versatile, works over suits and with casual trousers. Knee-length and below reads as more formal and suits taller frames best. If you are unsure, contact us with your height and intended use before ordering and we will advise.

**Q:** Can I choose the lining and color of my coat?  
**A:** Yes. Most styles are available in multiple outer fabric colors, and lining options vary by coat. The peacoat collection offers 33 lining color choices. Some styles offer standard acetate lining; others allow bespoke lining selection. The specific options for each coat are listed on its individual product page.

**Q:** How long does production and delivery take?  
**A:** Production is typically 4 to 6 weeks from order confirmation. This covers making the test coat, shipping it to you, receiving your fit confirmation, and completing the final garment. Worldwide tracked shipping is included in the price. If you have a fixed deadline — a wedding, convention, or event — mention it at checkout and we will advise on feasibility.
