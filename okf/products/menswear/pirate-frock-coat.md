**Q:** How long does a well-made velvet frock coat last compared to a costume-shop version?  
**A:** A bespoke velvet frock coat built on a canvas front with quality poly-cotton velvet will last 15 to 20 years with correct care, where a costume-grade polyester version typically loses its pile and shape after three to five wears. A heavier poly-cotton velvet with deeper pile holds its appearance through repeated use. The canvas front matters too — it maintains the coat's silhouette through every wearing rather than collapsing like a fused front does after dry cleaning.

**Q:** Why does velvet pill or flatten, and how does this coat avoid that?  
**A:** Velvet piles flatten when the fibers are too short, too fine, or made from synthetic materials that compress under pressure. Polyester velvet used in costume-grade coats has a shallow pile that flattens in the first few wears and cannot be lifted back. The premium poly-cotton velvet used in this coat has a deeper pile and a denser weave, which holds its texture longer and responds better to steaming when needed. Dry cleaning is still required — velvet should never be washed — but the pile recovery after a proper dry clean is significantly better in a quality velvet than in costume fabric.
