## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why does an off-the-rack dress shirt never fit properly?  
**A:** Because a ready-made shirt is sized on only two measurements, the neck and the sleeve length, while the chest, waist, shoulder, and cuff are all cut to a fixed average. That means the shirt that fits your neck usually billows through the body, and the one cut slim enough for your body pulls tight at the collar. A shirt made to your measurements is cut to your neck, your body, and your arm at the same time, which is the only way to get all of them right.

**Q:** What is Egyptian cotton, and what does 220 thread count mean?  
**A:** Egyptian cotton is a long-staple cotton whose longer fibers spin into a finer, stronger, smoother yarn than ordinary cotton, which is why it feels softer and lasts longer. A 220 thread count means 220 threads woven per square inch, a fine, dense weave that gives the shirt a smooth hand and a subtle sheen without becoming heavy. Together they make Egyptian cotton the benchmark for an everyday luxury dress shirt, and it is the cloth most of our cotton shirts are made from.

**Q:** Is a raw silk shirt worth it compared to cotton?  
**A:** A raw silk shirt is worth it if you want a shirt that looks and feels distinctly different from cotton. Raw silk has a matte, slightly textured surface that catches light softly and reads as considered and unusual rather than standard, and it drapes with a weight cotton does not have. It is best for evenings, events, and occasions where you want to stand out, while cotton remains the more practical choice for heavy daily wear. Both are made to your measurements here.

**Q:** What is the difference between a raw silk shirt and a satin silk shirt?  
**A:** Raw silk has a matte, textured, slubbed surface, while satin silk is smooth and luminous with a visible sheen. Raw silk reads as understated and tactile, ideal for a distinctive but not flashy look, whereas satin silk is the dressier, more formal of the two, at home at a black-tie event or under a tuxedo. Both are 100% natural silk and both are cut to your measurements, so the choice comes down to whether you want texture or shine.

**Q:** Are silk shirts practical to wear and care for?  
**A:** Silk shirts are practical when cared for correctly, and many men wear them regularly. Hand wash or machine wash on a delicate cycle in cold water, hang to dry, and steam rather than press with a hot iron, and a silk shirt will stay beautiful for a long time. Silk is naturally breathable and temperature-regulating, which makes it comfortable across seasons, though for very heavy daily wear a 220-thread Egyptian cotton is the more hard-wearing choice.

**Q:** What collar and cuff options can I choose?  
**A:** You choose the collar style and the cuff on every shirt, so it suits your face, your ties, and how you wear it. Collar options run from a classic point to a spread or cutaway, and cuffs can be button cuffs for everyday wear or French cuffs for cufflinks and formal occasions. You can also specify the placket, whether the shirt has a chest pocket, and the overall fit, all as part of the same made-to-your-measurements order.

**Q:** What measurements do you need for a shirt?  
**A:** We need your neck, chest, waist, shoulder width, sleeve length, and shirt length, all entered on the product page with a short guide for each. If you have a shirt at home that fits the way you like, you can measure it flat and match those numbers, which many customers find easiest. Because the shirt is cut to all of these rather than just neck and sleeve, both the collar and the body come out right.

**Q:** Do you make linen shirts for summer or a beach wedding?  
**A:** Yes, our pure linen shirts are made to your measurements and are ideal for summer, warm climates, and beach or destination weddings. Linen breathes better than any other shirting fabric, which keeps you cool through a hot day, and it pairs naturally with a linen suit or stands on its own with the sleeves rolled. It creases by nature, which reads as relaxed and appropriate in a warm-weather setting.

**Q:** How should a dress shirt fit?  
**A:** A dress shirt should be close through the body without pulling at the buttons, sit flat across the shoulders with the seam at the edge of your shoulder, and end with about a finger of room at the collar when buttoned. The sleeve should reach the base of the thumb so a little cuff shows under a jacket, and the shirt should be long enough to stay tucked when you move. A made-to-your-measurements shirt is built to hit all of these at once, which is what an off-the-rack shirt cannot do.

**Q:** Are these shirts made to my measurements or standard sizes?  
**A:** Every shirt here is made to your measurements, not sold in standard collar sizes. You choose the fabric, collar, cuff, and fit, then enter your measurements, and the shirt is cut and hand-finished to those numbers. There is no S, M, or L, and nothing pre-made, which is the reason the fit is correct through the neck and the body at the same time.

**Q:** How long does a shirt take, and do you ship worldwide?  
**A:** A made-to-your-measurements shirt typically takes a few weeks from order to dispatch, and we confirm the current timeline when you order. We ship worldwide by trackable courier from Dubai, and you receive tracking as soon as your shirt ships. If you have several shirts to order or an event date, tell us and we will confirm the timing.

**Q:** Can I order several shirts at once in different fabrics?  
**A:** Yes, and many customers do, because once your measurements are set they apply to every shirt you order. A common approach is a few 220-thread Egyptian cotton shirts for daily wear, a linen shirt for summer, and a raw silk or satin silk shirt for evenings, all cut to the same fit. Order each on its product page with the fabric, collar, and cuff you want for that shirt.
