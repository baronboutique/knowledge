Most men buy trousers as an afterthought to the jacket, off a rack, in a waist-and-length size that ignores the rise, the seat, and the thigh entirely. A jacket hides a lot; trousers hide nothing, because they are seen from every angle when you stand, sit, and walk. This is exactly why made-to-measure trousers are the single highest-impact upgrade in a wardrobe, and the least expensive place to start.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** How should men&#8217;s trousers fit?  
**A:** Well-fitted trousers sit at a comfortable rise where the waistband stays put without a belt straining, follow the seat and thigh cleanly without pulling or sagging, and finish at the shoe with the break you prefer. The rise, the seat, and the thigh are the three measurements off-the-rack trousers get wrong most often, because they are cut to a size block rather than your body. Made-to-measure trousers are built from your actual rise, seat, and leg, which is why they solve the fit problems a belt and a quick hem never can.

**Q:** What is trouser break, and which should I choose?  
**A:** Break is the fold of fabric where the trouser hem meets the shoe. A full break has the fabric resting fully on the shoe with a clear fold, a traditional and relaxed look. A half or medium break shows a slight fold and is the most versatile, modern choice. No break, where the hem just touches or sits above the shoe, is the sharpest and most contemporary. You choose your break when you set the trouser length, and confirm it on the free test garment before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Should I choose pleats or a flat front?  
**A:** A flat front is cleaner and more modern and flatters a slim to average build, while pleats add room through the seat and thigh and can be more comfortable and traditional, particularly for a fuller build or for men who sit a lot. Because the trousers are made to your measurements, the choice is genuinely yours rather than dictated by what a rack had in stock. Specify flat front or single or double pleats on the product page.

**Q:** Can I buy trousers on their own to match a jacket I already have?  
**A:** Yes, that is exactly what these are built for. Each pair is cut on its own trouser block to your measurements, so they work as true separates rather than only as suit bottoms. Choose a cloth and color that complements an existing blazer or sport coat, grey flannel and charcoal worsted are the most versatile partners, and specify the details to match. It is the most economical way to get a tailored look from jackets you already own.

It pairs naturally with a linen grooms&#8217; suit or a fine cotton or silk shirt. Order well ahead of the date so there is time for the free test garment and any adjustments.

**Q:** What is the difference between grey flannel and charcoal worsted trousers?  
**A:** Flannel is a soft, brushed wool with a slightly fuzzy surface that feels warm and looks relaxed, ideal for autumn and winter and for pairing with knitwear and tweed. Charcoal and grey worsted, including sharkskin and merino, is a smoother, harder-finished cloth that presses to a sharper crease and reads more formal, suited to year-round office and dress wear. Many men own both: flannel for cold-weather softness and worsted for a crisp, formal line.

**Q:** Are the trousers made to my measurements, and is there a fit guarantee?  
**A:** Yes to both. Every pair is made to your measurements with no standard sizes, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test garment in a low-cost fabric so the fit is confirmed on your body first. You try it on at home, check the waist, rise, seat, and break, and tell us any adjustments, which we apply before cutting the final trousers. The test garment is yours to keep. This is how we guarantee fit for customers ordering from anywhere in the world.

**Q:** Can I choose a cuff or a plain hem?  
**A:** Yes. You can choose a plain hem for a clean, modern finish or a turn-up cuff for a more traditional, structured look that also adds a little weight to help the trouser hang. Cuffs suit flannel and heavier cloths and a fuller break, while a plain hem suits dress trousers and a sharper, no-break finish. Specify your preference on the product page, and it is confirmed on the free test garment.

**Q:** How do I care for tailored trousers?  
**A:** Dry clean wool, silk, and flannel trousers rather than washing them, and press to restore the crease when needed, using a cloth over silk and flannel to protect the surface. Hang trousers by the hem in a clamp hanger or folded over a bar so the crease stays sharp and the knees do not bag. Rotate pairs rather than wearing the same trousers on consecutive days, which lets the cloth recover. Linen is more relaxed and can be pressed crisp before an event.
