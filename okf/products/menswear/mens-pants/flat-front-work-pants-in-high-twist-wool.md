## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What exactly is high-twist wool and why does it resist creasing better than regular wool?  
**A:** High-twist wool is defined by how the yarn is made, not how the fabric is labeled. In standard woolen yarn, fibers are twisted together under low tension. In a high-twist yarn, that tension is significantly increased - the fibers are wound tighter, storing more energy. When woven into an open-weave structure, the result is a cloth that physically springs back against compression rather than accepting it. The open weave also creates air channels through the cloth, making it breathable in warm conditions. This structural performance does not degrade over time - it is not a chemical finish, it is the construction of the yarn itself.

**Q:** Can the trouser balance and seat be adjusted if the test trouser is not right?  
**A:** Yes - and this is exactly why the test trouser exists. Trouser balance, seat height, thigh room, and rise are structural properties set in the pattern. They cannot be corrected through standard alteration once a garment is finished - a tailor can only work at the seam edges. Most people do not realize this when they buy off-the-rack trousers and have them altered. The test phase exists so that all structural elements are confirmed and refined before any final fabric is cut.

**Q:** Are these trousers suitable as summer or year-round work pants?  
**A:** High-twist wool in the Super 130s grade is one of the best year-round professional fabrics available. The open-weave structure breathes well enough for warm-weather office environments, while wool's natural temperature-regulation properties make it comfortable in air-conditioned settings. Many tailoring houses use high-twist wool specifically for clients who need a single trouser that works across seasons. For very hot climates or outdoor summer wear, a linen or lighter tropical wool might be preferred - but for professional office and travel use year-round, this construction performs exceptionally.

**Q:** Why is the trouser lined only to the knee rather than fully lined?  
**A:** Acetate-lined to the front knee is the correct construction for a performance dress trouser. Full lining traps heat through the lower leg and restricts the natural movement of the cloth. Lining to the knee provides the practical benefits - a smooth slide when putting on the trouser, protection of the cloth against friction at the thigh - without compromising breathability below the knee. It is also the standard used by tailoring houses that build trousers to perform and last, rather than trousers built to feel substantial on a hanger.
