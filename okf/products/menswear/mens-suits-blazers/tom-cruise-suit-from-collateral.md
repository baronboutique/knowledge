## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What makes the Collateral suit different from a standard single-button grey suit?  
**A:** Several details mark this suit as specific to the Collateral character rather than a generic single-button suit. The lapels are narrow, between 2.75 and 3.25 inches, which was unusual at the time the film was made and remains distinctive. All three pockets are piped with no flap, giving the front a very clean line. The trouser waistband uses side-tab adjusters with no belt loops, a detail that most off-the-rack single-button suits do not carry. The double vents, rounded jacket hem, and single working sleeve cuff complete the construction. Taken together these details produce a suit that a knowledgeable observer recognizes immediately and everyone else simply reads as exceptionally well-dressed.

**Q:** Why does canvas front construction matter for this specific suit?  
**A:** A fused front jacket uses adhesive to bond the facing to the outer fabric. Over time and dry cleaning, the adhesive breaks down and the fabric bubbles. You will not know it has happened until it has happened, and it cannot be corrected. A canvas front uses a layer of woven horsehair canvas floating between the outer fabric and the lining. It molds to the wearer's chest over time and holds its shape indefinitely. For the Collateral suit specifically, where the entire aesthetic depends on a clean, flat jacket front with no visual interruption, a fused front that eventually bubbles destroys the suit. Canvas front is not a luxury upgrade here. It is the correct construction for a suit that is supposed to last.

**Q:** Can I wear this as an everyday suit rather than a film reference?  
**A:** Yes, and this is increasingly the reason people order it. The Collateral suit is a study in restraint. Single button, narrow notch lapels, no decorative pockets, clean front, beltless trousers. In light grey, it reads as a very well-made minimalist suit to anyone who does not recognize the reference. In any of the other 11 available colors it moves further from the character association and closer to a distinctive everyday suit. It works for formal business settings, events where a suit is required, occasions where you want to be well-dressed without making a statement about it. The construction is built for repeated wear.
