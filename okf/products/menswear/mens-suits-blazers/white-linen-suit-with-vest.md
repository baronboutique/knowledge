## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why is a white linen suit with vest harder to get right off the rack than a regular suit?  
**A:** Three reasons compound on each other. First, white fabric is merciless — there is no dark color to minimize the visual impact of a shoulder that sits wide or a chest that pulls. Second, linen amplifies fit issues because its drape is entirely dependent on how the garment was cut; unlike structured wool, linen has nothing internal to compensate for a bad fit. Third, the vest in a three-piece must fit a different part of your body than the jacket — your torso length, your chest-to-waist ratio, your shoulder width — and off-the-rack sets are sized as one unit. The result is almost always a vest that is visibly wrong even when the jacket looks acceptable.

**Q:** How does the free test suit work for a three-piece?  
**A:** When you place your order, you enter your measurements on the product page. We build a complete three-piece test suit — jacket, vest, and trousers — in a plain fabric and ship it by trackable courier. You try on all three pieces at home. You check each one: jacket chest and shoulders, vest button stance and back fit, trouser waist and break. You tell us anything that needs adjusting — even small details like a quarter-inch on the trouser hem or a centimeter on the vest length. We apply every change before cutting your linen. The test suit is yours to keep once your commission is complete.

**Q:** Will linen wrinkle during a long event?  
**A:** Linen creases naturally — that is part of the fabric's character. What matters is whether those creases look intentional or accidental. A white linen suit with vest cut to your measurements creases along the lines of your body, which reads as relaxed and natural. A suit that doesn't fit creases unevenly and randomly, which reads as sloppy. The fit is what determines whether linen looks considered or careless.
