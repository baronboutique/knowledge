## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is Vitale Barberis Canonico and why does it matter?  
**A:** Vitale Barberis Canonico is an Italian wool mill based in Biella, a region of northern Italy that has been the center of European fine textile production for centuries. The mill has been operating since 1663 and is currently run by the thirteenth generation of the same family. VBC supplies fabric to many of the most respected tailors and suit houses in Europe. When a suit is described as using VBC cloth, it means the fabric was woven at that specific mill to their quality standards - it is a verifiable origin claim, not just a marketing label. Super 160s from VBC specifically refers to a fine-grade worsted wool with a luminous surface quality and a smooth, precise drape.

**Q:** What does Super 160s mean and how is it different from Super 120s or Super 100s?  
**A:** The Super number refers to the fineness of the wool yarn. The higher the number, the finer the individual fibers, the smoother the fabric surface, and the more precise the drape. Super 100s is a good working wool - durable, reliable, used in quality ready-to-wear. Super 120s is finer, with a noticeably softer hand. Super 160s is finer still - the grade where the cloth has a luminous quality under light, where the surface feels smooth rather than textured, and where the drape is sharp and precise. The trade-off at higher grades is that finer wools require more careful handling and are less forgiving of poor fit - which is exactly why the free test suit process matters more, not less, at this quality level.
