## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why does Super 130s worsted wool look better than cheaper black suit fabrics?  
**A:** Most off-the-rack black suits are blended with polyester to reduce cost and add wrinkle resistance. Polyester reflects light differently from wool and the blended fabric reads as slightly shiny under artificial lighting. The black color appears less deep. Super 130s plain worsted wool absorbs light evenly across the surface and holds the depth of the black in all lighting conditions, including the mixed artificial light of indoor events and the flash of a camera. The difference is most visible in photographs. A man in Super 130s plain worsted wool and a man in a polyester-wool blend look different in the same photograph. Most men do not realize they are the man in the cheaper suit until they see the image.

**Q:** What is a full canvas front and why does it matter for a black suit?  
**A:** A fused front jacket uses adhesive to bond the facing to the outer fabric. It holds its shape when new. Over dry cleaning cycles, the adhesive breaks down and the jacket front bubbles. For a black suit specifically, any surface irregularity is immediately visible against the plain, uninterrupted surface of the cloth. A full canvas front uses a layer of horsehair canvas floating between the outer fabric and the lining. It does not bubble, molds to the wearer's chest over time, and holds its clean flat front indefinitely. For a suit you intend to wear regularly for years, full canvas is not a luxury addition. It is the correct construction.
