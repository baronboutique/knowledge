What Dupioni Silk Does That Other Silks Cannot
Dupioni silk is woven from two silk filaments from different cocoons wound together on the same thread. Where they intersect unevenly, the weave produces a natural cross-grain slub — a small ridge of slightly thicker yarn running across the width of the fabric. In most colors, these slubs read as texture. In black, they do something else: they create depth. A plain-woven black fabric reflects light uniformly, producing a flat, matte surface. Black dupioni silk catches and scatters light differently across the slubbed areas, giving the fabric a dimensional quality that photographs with a richness that plain silk, polyester satin, or wool-silk blends cannot replicate.

Why the Vertical Grain Matters for a Suit
We cut the fiber grain vertically on every jacket and trouser panel. This is not the only way to cut dupioni silk — some makers cut it on the bias or diagonally to reduce cost — but it is the correct way for a suit. The vertical grain aligns the slubs horizontally across the body, which reinforces the structure of the fabric under the canvas and allows the jacket to drape cleanly from the shoulder without torquing or pulling. A black dupioni silk suit cut on the wrong grain will show that decision every time you wear it.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is dupioni silk and why does it look different from other black suits?  
**A:** Dupioni silk is woven from two silk filaments from separate cocoons wound together on the same thread. Where the filaments meet unevenly, the weave produces natural cross-grain slubs — small ridges of thicker yarn running across the fabric width. In black, these slubs scatter light differently from the surrounding weave, giving the fabric a dimensional depth that plain-woven silk, wool, or synthetic alternatives cannot replicate. A black dupioni silk suit reads as intentionally rich rather than simply dark.

**Q:** What does "3-button roll to 2" actually mean, and why does it matter?  
**A:** A 3-roll-2 suit has three buttons on the jacket front, but only two are functional — the middle and bottom. The top button sits above the natural lapel break point and is never fastened. The lapel rolls back to the middle button, creating a longer, more formal V-line than a standard 2-button suit. The roll is not a styling choice you can apply to any jacket. It is a construction outcome determined by how the canvas is pad-stitched beneath the lapel. Without a canvas front, the lapel cannot roll correctly — it sits flat and the defining characteristic of the suit disappears.

**Q:** Most "3-roll-2" suits I've seen have flat lapels. Why?  
**A:** Because most of them are built with fused interlining rather than a canvas front. A fused suit glues the interlining to the face fabric with heat-activated adhesive. This produces a flat, stiff front that cannot hold a genuine roll — the lapel is pressed into a shape rather than built to hold one. Over time, especially with dry cleaning and body heat, the adhesive weakens and the lapel may crease or spring unpredictably. A canvas front is pad-stitched by hand to hold the break at the exact point that works for your chest measurement. That roll is permanent and improves as the canvas softens to your body.

**Q:** The fabric has small ridges or marks in it — are those defects?  
**A:** No. Those are the slubs — the defining characteristic of authentic dupioni silk. They are caused by the double-filament weaving process and appear throughout every yard of genuine dupioni. A smooth, uniform surface would indicate the fabric is not dupioni. The slubs become more refined and subtle with wear as the silk settles.

**Q:** Does dupioni silk wrinkle easily?  
**A:** Dupioni is one of the more wrinkle-resistant silks because of its structured, medium-weight hand. Minor creasing from sitting or travel typically falls out within an hour of hanging. For persistent creases, hang the suit in a steamy bathroom — the moisture releases the silk without direct heat. Never iron dupioni silk directly. For travel, fold along the natural seam lines and hang immediately upon arrival.
