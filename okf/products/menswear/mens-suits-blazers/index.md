A Suit Is the One Garment Where Off-the-Rack Fit Fails Most Visibly
More than any other garment, a suit exposes a bad fit. The shoulder is the hardest part of a jacket to alter and the first thing the eye reads, so a shoulder built for an average body sits wrong on yours no matter what a tailor does afterward. The chest pulls, the collar gaps, the waist is boxy, and the trouser break is whatever the size block decided. This is why an expensive off-the-rack suit can still look cheap, and why a made-to-your-measurements suit at a fraction of bespoke prices can look like far more than it cost.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** How should a men&#8217;s suit fit?  
**A:** A suit jacket should sit cleanly on the shoulders with the seam ending where your shoulder ends, close the chest without pulling or gaping at the collar, and finish long enough to cover the seat of the trousers. The sleeve should show about a half-inch of shirt cuff, and the trousers should break lightly at the shoe with no pooling. The shoulder is the critical measurement because it is the hardest area to alter, which is why a suit made to your measurements, and confirmed on a free test suit before the cloth is cut, fits in a way an altered off-the-rack suit rarely can.

**Q:** What is the difference between made-to-measure and off-the-rack?  
**A:** An off-the-rack suit is cut to a standard size block, an average of many bodies, and then optionally altered, but the shoulder and chest structure cannot really be re-engineered after the fact. A made-to-measure suit like ours starts from your measurements, so the shoulders, chest, waist, jacket length, and trouser break are built for you from the start. It costs far less than traditional bespoke, and the free test suit removes the fit risk that normally comes with ordering tailoring online.

**Q:** How does the free test suit work?  
**A:** When you order, you enter your measurements on the product page. Before we cut your chosen cloth, we build a complete test suit in a low-cost fabric to those measurements and ship it to you by trackable courier. You try it on at home, check the fit, and send us any adjustments. We update your pattern and only then cut your final suit. The test suit is yours to keep. It is the single step that lets us guarantee fit for customers ordering from anywhere in the world.

**Q:** What fabrics can I choose for a suit?  
**A:** The range includes 100% dupioni and raw silk, a Super 130s silk-and-wool blend, VBC and tropical wool, high-twist all-season worsted, Super 160s cloth, mohair blends, and lightweight cloths for unstructured suits. Silk suits are the most-requested for weddings and events; wool and high-twist worsted are the everyday and travel choices; mohair adds a subtle formal sheen. Each product page lists the cloths and colors available for that piece, and custom cloth requests can be discussed before ordering.

**Q:** Do you make pinstripe and chalk-stripe suits?  
**A:** Yes, in grey, navy, brown, and white grounds with pinstripe or chalk-stripe patterning, all made to your measurements. A pinstripe reads as sharp and formal and works well for the office or a power look, while chalk stripe is softer and slightly more relaxed. Because the suit is cut to your body, the stripes stay vertical and true rather than distorting across a poorly fitted chest, which is where cheap striped suiting usually fails.

**Q:** What is an unstructured suit, and who is it for?  
**A:** An unstructured or soft-tailored suit is built with little or no shoulder padding and minimal internal canvas, so it feels more like a shirt-jacket and drapes naturally from the body. It is lighter, more comfortable in heat, and packs better for travel, which makes it ideal for warm climates and for men who find a fully structured suit stiff. The trade-off is a softer shoulder line rather than a sharp, formal one, so a structured suit remains the better choice for strict business or black-tie settings.

**Q:** Two-piece or three-piece, and can I add a waistcoat?  
**A:** Most suits are offered as a two-piece, with a matching waistcoat available to make it a three-piece for an additional charge. A three-piece adds formality and a layered look that works well for weddings and colder weather. Add the waistcoat in the order notes at checkout and we will confirm the details before production, or browse the separate Vests collection.

**Q:** Can I choose the lapel, buttons, vents, and lining?  
**A:** Yes. You can specify notch, peak, or shawl lapel, the button count and stance, single or double vents, a ticket pocket, and the lining color, along with other details, in the customizer or the order notes. Because each suit is made to your measurements, these are genuine construction choices rather than fixed options. If you want something not listed, contact us before ordering and we will confirm what is possible.

**Q:** Which suit is best for a wedding?  
**A:** For a groom or guest, a dupioni silk suit photographs beautifully and reads as special, while a clean navy or grey wool suit is the versatile choice a guest will wear again. For a distinctive groom look, Baron&#8217;s wedding collection includes the signature wedding suit with a cape and linen grooms&#8217; suits. All are made to your measurements with a free test suit, so the fit is confirmed before the day. See the Wedding Suits collection.

**Q:** How long does a suit take, and how far ahead should I order?  
**A:** Production is about four to six weeks from the point your measurements are confirmed, including the free test-suit stage. For a fixed date such as a wedding, order at least 8 to 10 weeks ahead so there is comfortable time for the test suit and any adjustments before the final suit is made and shipped. If your date is sooner, contact us before ordering and we will tell you honestly whether the timeline works.

**Q:** How much does a custom suit cost here?  
**A:** Blazers start at $569, most made-to-your-measurements suits run $699 to $799 depending on cloth, and elaborate pieces such as the cape wedding suit go higher. The free test suit and made-to-measure construction are included at no extra charge. For comparison, a comparable garment from a traditional local bespoke tailor typically costs several times more, while an off-the-rack suit at a similar price offers no fit guarantee.

**Q:** Do you ship worldwide, and how do I pay?  
**A:** Yes, we ship worldwide from Dubai by trackable courier, usually 3 to 7 business days once your suit is finished.
