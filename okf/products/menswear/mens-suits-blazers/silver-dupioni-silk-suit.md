Why dupioni photographs differently from any other fabric
Dupioni is woven from the reeled thread of two silkworms whose cocoons merge during spinning. The result is a natural horizontal slub - an irregular crosswise texture that cannot be replicated in plain-weave or synthetic silk. It is this slub that gives dupioni its directional light response: the cloth holds light differently at every angle, which is why it does not look the same in two photographs taken at the same event from different positions. In silver, that property is most visible. Restrained and cool in natural daylight. Luminous under the warm light of a ceremony or reception hall.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Is a gray dupioni silk suit the same as a silver dupioni silk suit?  
**A:** Yes. Silver and gray describe the same cloth under different light. In natural daylight, silver dupioni reads as cool steel grey. Under the warm directional lighting at a wedding venue or formal event, the same cloth shifts toward luminous silver. Both search terms describe this product. If you are searching for a gray dupioni silk suit for a wedding, this is it.

**Q:** What makes dupioni silk look different from other silk?  
**A:** Dupioni is woven from the reeled thread of two silkworms whose cocoons merge during spinning. This produces a natural horizontal slub - an irregular crosswise texture - that no flat-weave or synthetic silk can replicate. The slub gives the fabric its directional light response: it reads differently from every angle and under every light source. This is why dupioni photographs with depth that polyester and plain-weave silk cannot produce.

**Q:** What is the difference between horizontal and vertical slub in dupioni?  
**A:** In a standard dupioni suit, the slub runs horizontally across the body. Light catches it laterally as you move, adding texture that reads as depth from the front. Baron has made dupioni suits with the slub running vertically - the texture falls with the garment instead of across it. The visual effect is elongating and architectural. In black dupioni, vertical slub changes the entire character of the cloth. See the black dupioni 3-roll-2 suit for an example.

**Q:** What is the 5-roll-2 button stance?  
**A:** A 5-roll-2 jacket has five buttons on the front placket, but the lapel rolls naturally to the second button. Only the lower two or three buttons are ever fastened. The effect is a longer lapel roll than a standard two-button jacket - the roll point sits higher on the chest, making the silhouette more vertical and more formal. It is the configuration tailors choose for themselves when they want a single-breasted suit that reads as genuinely tailored, not default.

**Q:** How do I care for a dupioni silk suit?  
**A:** Dry-clean only. Do not spot-clean with water - dupioni marks easily from direct moisture contact. Hang on a wide suit hanger between wears and allow it to breathe. The natural slub hides minor surface creases better than plain-weave silk does. Store in a breathable garment bag between seasons. With proper care, a dupioni silk suit holds its structure and character for many years.
