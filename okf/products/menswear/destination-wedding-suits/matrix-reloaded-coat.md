## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is the difference between the Matrix Reloaded coat and other Neo coats?  
**A:** The Matrix Reloaded coat is specifically the coat Neo wears from Matrix 2 onward, it is distinct from the shorter coat in the original film and from the coat in Matrix Resurrections. The Reloaded version is defined by its below-ankle length, mandarin collar (1.25 to 1.75 inches tall), 17 fabric-covered buttons with loop fastening, princess seam back with center kick pleat, and pleated flared coat skirt. Most sellers conflate these versions or use a generic long coat pattern. This coat is built from the Reloaded-specific pattern.

**Q:** Why monks wool instead of leather or PVC like in the film?  
**A:** The screen-used coats were made from a wool-based fabric, not leather or PVC. What looks black and slightly reflective on screen is monks wool , a tightly woven, matte wool fabric with a subtle sheen that reads as dark and cinematic under lighting. Leather and PVC versions exist in the market but they drape differently and read as costume rather than tailored outerwear. Monks wool gives you the correct silhouette, the correct weight for movement, and a finish that works both at conventions and as everyday outerwear. It is also significantly more comfortable to wear for extended periods than any synthetic alternative.

**Q:** Can I wear this as an everyday coat, not just for cosplay?  
**A:** Yes, and this is increasingly the reason people order it. The Matrix aesthetic has been on major fashion runways continuously since the early 2000s, and the Reloaded silhouette specifically has appeared in designer collections from Dior, Saint Laurent, and Balenciaga. In black monks wool, this coat reads as a dramatic statement coat to anyone who does not recognize the character reference. It is appropriate for formal events, film premieres, editorial shoots, theatrical occasions, and anywhere a deliberately dramatic long coat works. It is not appropriate for casual daily errands, the below-ankle length and construction demand an occasion.

**Q:** How does the free test coat process work?  
**A:** You enter your measurements on the product page when you order. We make a complete test coat in cotton to your measurements, including the full pattern with mandarin collar, button positions, back construction, and pleated skirt, and ship it to you via trackable courier. You try it on, check the hem length at the back, the shoulder line, the chest, and how it moves. You send us your adjustment notes. We correct every note in the pattern and then cut your final coat in monks wool. The test coat is yours to keep. This step matters especially for a below-ankle coat, where the hem landing point on your specific frame has a significant effect on how the silhouette reads.

**Q:** How many buttons does the coat have?  
**A:** The standard configuration is 17 fabric-covered buttons. The exact count can vary slightly depending on your coat length, the buttons are spaced to maintain the screen inspired proportions, so a slightly shorter or longer coat on a taller or shorter frame may have different button counts.
