## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Which season of the Fourth Doctor's costume is this?  
**A:** This is the Season 16-17 frock coat -- the long beige coat with brown velvet collar and brown cotton piping that Tom Baker wore through some of his most celebrated stories. It is the coat most associated with his era and the hardest version to find done correctly. The earlier Season 12 safari jacket and the later Season 18 coat are different garments entirely.

**Q:** Why does the off-center back vent matter?  
**A:** The back vent on the screen-worn coat overlaps off-center rather than splitting down the middle. On every cheap replica it is dead center. It is a small detail that anyone who has studied the coat will notice immediately -- especially from behind at a convention. We build it off-center because that is how the original was made.

**Q:** What does the princess seam back do?  
**A:** Princess seams run vertically through the back of the coat rather than relying on side seams alone. They allow the coat to be shaped through the back -- fitted across the shoulders and flared through the skirt -- which is what gives this coat its distinctive silhouette. A flat back without princess seams hangs straight and loses the shape entirely. Most cheap versions skip them because they add complexity to cut and construct.

**Q:** Can I get this in a color other than beige?  
**A:** Yes. The coat is available in 11 wool colors including tan, light grey, grey, charcoal, navy, red, brown, black, dark grey, and blue. You can also choose your collar trim from velvet and corduroy options in multiple colors, and your lining from 34 acetate colors. Price does not vary by color or trim choice.

**Q:** Can I specify a custom coat length?  
**A:** Yes. Note your preferred coat length through the measurement page during checkout. The test coat process also gives you the opportunity to check the length on your body before the final coat is made.

**Q:** How long does production take?  
**A:** Allow 3-4 weeks from test coat approval to delivery of the final coat. This covers production of the test coat, shipping it to you, any adjustments, and production of the final garment. Both ship worldwide by trackable courier.

**Q:** Is this coat wearable outside of conventions?  
**A:** Yes. Made in Super 130s worsted wool with a canvas front, it wears as a proper long frock coat -- not a costume. The silhouette is dramatic and intentional. Several customers order it purely as a statement coat with no convention in mind. The character reference is context, not a requirement.
