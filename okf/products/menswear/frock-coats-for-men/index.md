## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is a frock coat?  
**A:** A frock coat is a knee-length men&#8217;s coat with a horizontal waist seam that gives it a fitted body and a full, skirt-like lower section. It was the standard daytime coat for men through most of the nineteenth century and remains the most formal and dramatic coat silhouette in classic menswear. Unlike a modern overcoat, which hangs straight, a frock coat is shaped at the waist, which is exactly what gives the wearer such a striking, upright line. Ours are built as genuine tailoring in real cloth, not as costume pieces.

**Q:** What is the difference between a frock coat, a tailcoat, and a morning coat?  
**A:** All three are nineteenth-century formal coats, and the difference is in the skirt. A frock coat has a full skirt all the way around, front and back, cut level at the knee. A tailcoat is cut away sharply at the front waist, leaving only the two tails at the back, and is worn for white-tie evening dress. A morning coat, sometimes called a cutaway, curves gradually from the front waist to the tails at the back and is the daytime formal coat still worn at weddings and Ascot. The frock coat is the fullest and most imposing of the three.

**Q:** What era is the frock coat from?  
**A:** The frock coat dominated men&#8217;s daywear from roughly the 1820s through the end of the Victorian era and into the Edwardian years, before the shorter lounge suit and morning coat displaced it in the early twentieth century. That history is why it is central to Victorian, Regency-adjacent, Dickensian, steampunk, and gothic dress, and to period reenactment. Baron&#8217;s coats reference that heritage but are cut to fit and move on a modern body.

**Q:** How do you wear a frock coat today?  
**A:** For a period, steampunk, or gothic look, wear it over a waistcoat and high-collared shirt with a cravat and tailored trousers. For a themed or vintage wedding, a linen or wool frock coat over a waistcoat reads as elegant rather than costume. And for a bold everyday statement, a subdued wool frock coat in navy or Oxford blue can be worn over a plain shirt and trousers the way you would wear a long dramatic overcoat. The key is fit and proportion, which is why made-to-measure matters so much on this silhouette.

**Q:** Is a frock coat only for cosplay and reenactment?  
**A:** No, though it excels there. Because these are real tailored garments in wool, velvet, and linen rather than costume-grade polyester, they work for cosplay and historical reenactment that demands accuracy, but equally for a themed or vintage wedding, a theatrical or operatic event, or simply as a dramatic statement coat. The Doctor Who and Sherlock Holmes coats in particular are screen-accurate yet built to be worn well beyond a convention.

**Q:** Can I wear a frock coat to a wedding?  
**A:** Yes, and it makes a distinctive groom&#8217;s look. A linen frock coat suits a summer or destination wedding without overheating, while a wool or velvet frock coat suits a cooler, vintage, or gothic-themed ceremony. Worn over a matching waistcoat and tailored trousers it reads as considered period elegance. Because each is made to your measurements with a free test coat, the fit is confirmed before the day, and you can coordinate it with pieces from the Wedding Suits collection.

**Q:** How long should a frock coat be?  
**A:** A classic frock coat falls to around the top of the knee or just below, with the waist seam sitting at your natural waist. Too short and it loses the dramatic skirt that defines the silhouette; too long and it reads as a robe. Because the correct length depends on your height and proportions, you set your coat length on the product page and confirm it on the free test coat before we cut the final cloth, so the proportion is right for your body rather than a generic size.

**Q:** Are these real tailoring or costumes?  
**A:** Real tailoring. Each frock coat is cut from genuine cloth, wool, velvet, linen, or cotton, on a hand-basted canvas front with a true waist seam, hand-stitched armholes, working details, and a full lined skirt. That is the opposite of the flat, unshaped polyester costume coats sold for a single wear. The construction is why these coats hold their line, move properly, and last, and why reenactors and collectors choose them over fancy-dress alternatives.

**Q:** Are the frock coats made to my measurements?  
**A:** Yes. Every frock coat is made to your measurements with no standard sizes, and because the silhouette is shaped and dramatic, fit matters more here than on almost any other garment. You enter your measurements on the product page, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test coat so the fit and the line are confirmed on your body first. The test coat is yours to keep.

**Q:** Can I request historical accuracy or a custom specification?  
**A:** Yes. Each coat is cut with generous seam allowances and can be built to a specific brief, a particular era&#8217;s cut, a chosen lapel or collar, button count and placement, skirt length, and cloth. This matters for reenactors and for stage or screen productions that need accuracy. Add your requirements in the order notes or contact us before ordering, and we will confirm what is achievable and build to it.

**Q:** Do you ship frock coats to the UK and internationally?  
**A:** Yes. We ship worldwide from Dubai by trackable courier, including throughout the UK, where frock coats are especially popular for reenactment, weddings, and theatre. Delivery is usually 3 to 7 business days once the coat is finished, and production runs about four to six weeks from the point your measurements are confirmed, including the free test-coat stage.

**Q:** How do I care for a frock coat?  
**A:** Dry clean the wool, velvet, and cotton frock coats to preserve the structure of the canvas front and the shape of the skirt, and hang the coat on a broad shaped hanger between wears so the shoulders and waist keep their line. Velvet should be brushed gently with the pile and steamed rather than pressed, so the nap is never crushed. Linen will relax with wear and benefits from a light press before an event. Cared for this way, a made-to-measure frock coat lasts for many years.
