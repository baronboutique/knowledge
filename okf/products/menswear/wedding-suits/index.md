## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What should a groom wear to his wedding?  
**A:** A groom should wear something made to his measurements that suits the setting and stands one step apart from the guests. For a summer or beach ceremony that means a breathable linen suit, for a formal evening a tuxedo, and for a groom who wants to be unmistakable a cape suit or a statement piece. The single most important thing is fit, which is why every wedding suit here is made to your measurements and confirmed with a free test suit before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** What is a wedding suit with a cape?  
**A:** A wedding suit with a cape pairs a tailored suit or mandarin-collar tunic with a floor-length cape, usually lined in satin silk, worn over the shoulders and fastened at the chest. It is one of the most dramatic looks a groom can wear, because the cape photographs like architecture against sky, sea, or an open venue and sweeps when he walks. Baron&#8217;s cape wedding suit is made to your measurements in Super 130s Fresco wool, and it is the look grooms search for here more than any other.

**Q:** Are linen suits good for a summer or beach wedding?  
**A:** Linen is the best fabric for a summer or beach wedding because it breathes, photographs with a natural texture, and reads as chosen for the occasion rather than borrowed from the office. The one requirement is fit, since linen has no structure to hide an approximate cut, which is exactly why a made-to-your-measurements linen suit looks so much sharper than an off-the-rack one. Baron&#8217;s linen wedding suits come in 18 colors from powder blue and stone to white and navy.

**Q:** Should a groom wear a tuxedo or a suit to a wedding?  
**A:** A tuxedo is the right choice for an evening or black-tie wedding, while a suit is more appropriate for a daytime, garden, or beach ceremony. A tuxedo reads as the most formal option and gives the groom satin lapels and a dressier register, whereas a linen or wool suit is lighter and better suited to warm or outdoor settings. Both are available here made to your measurements, so the decision comes down to the time of day and the formality of the venue.

**Q:** Should the groom and groomsmen match?  
**A:** The groom should sit one or two shades apart from the groomsmen rather than wear an identical outfit, so he reads as the focal point in every photograph. A common approach is groomsmen in a lighter neutral with the groom a shade deeper, or the groom in a distinct piece such as a cape suit or a frock coat while the party wears coordinating suits. Because everything here is made to each person&#8217;s measurements, you can outfit the whole party in one coherent look while keeping the groom distinct.

**Q:** Should I rent or buy a wedding suit?  
**A:** Buy, because a wedding suit is the one garment where fit and quality show most and a rental is fitted in minutes to whatever is roughly your size. A made-to-your-measurements wedding suit fits your body correctly on the most photographed day of your life, and you keep it and wear it again for years. For the cost of two or three rentals across a lifetime of events, a bought suit is both the better-looking and the better-value choice.

**Q:** What color should a groom&#8217;s suit be?  
**A:** For most weddings a groom looks best in navy, charcoal, stone, or a considered color that sets him apart from the guests, with linen shades like powder blue, tan, and off-white best for summer and beach ceremonies. Black and midnight blue are the formal evening choices, and burgundy is the standout for a groom who wants color. Because every suit here is made to your measurements in a wide range of colors at the same price, the choice comes down to the setting and your palette rather than cost.

**Q:** Are these wedding suits made to my measurements or standard sizes?  
**A:** Every wedding suit here is made to your measurements, with no standard sizes and nothing pre-made. You enter your measurements on the product page, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test suit so the fit is confirmed on your body first. This is the step that separates a suit that fits from a rental that is close but never right.

**Q:** How far in advance should I order my wedding suit?  
**A:** Order at least 10 to 12 weeks before the wedding so there is comfortable time for the free test suit and any adjustments before the final suit is made and shipped. Production takes about four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, and international delivery adds several days at each stage. Tell us your wedding date when you order so we can confirm the timeline honestly.

**Q:** Can I order for a destination wedding or a wedding abroad?  
**A:** Yes, the entire process is remote, so you can order from anywhere for a wedding anywhere. You enter your measurements on the product page, we ship a free test suit to your home, you try it on and send notes, then we cut and ship the final suit to any address you choose. The test-suit step is what makes ordering for a distant wedding safe, because the fit is confirmed before you travel.

**Q:** Can I get a wedding suit that no one else will have?  
**A:** Yes, and it is what Baron is known for. Beyond classic linen and tuxedo options, the collection includes cape suits, ceremonial frock coats, and cinematic pieces made to your measurements as real garments rather than costumes. A groom who wants the day to be unmistakably his own can choose a silhouette, a color, and a fabric that no rental shop and no other guest will be wearing.
