## Questions & Answers

**Q:** How long does a linen wedding suit last?  
**A:** A linen suit built on a canvas front and made to your measurements lasts 15 to 20 years with dry cleaning after each wear. The canvas front is the reason: fused interlinings, used in most off-the-rack suits, delaminate after repeated cleaning and cause the chest to bubble and collapse, while a canvas front holds its shape indefinitely. Linen itself is one of the most durable natural fibers and softens rather than deteriorates with age, so a good linen suit actually improves over time.

**Q:** Are these suits made to my measurements or sold in standard sizes?  
**A:** Every suit here is made to your measurements, with no standard sizes and nothing pre-made. The jacket, waistcoat, and trousers are each cut from your numbers, which matters more for linen than for most fabrics because linen has no structure to disguise approximate sizing. Before we cut your linen, we send a free test suit so the fit is confirmed on your body first.

**Q:** What measurements do you need for a linen groom suit?  
**A:** We need your chest, waist, hip, shoulder, sleeve length, jacket length, and inseam, and the chest and shoulder matter most for a clean drape. You enter all of them on the product page when you order, with a measuring guide directly above the fields. If you are unsure, a local tailor can take them in about ten minutes, and the free test suit is there to catch anything before your linen is cut.

**Q:** What color linen suit should a groom wear for a wedding?  
**A:** For most weddings a groom looks best in stone, tan, beige, light grey, or a soft blue, with white and cream best reserved for beach and destination ceremonies. Navy and dark blue are the most formal linen options and hold their depth even in bright daylight. All 18 colors in this collection are the same fabric and the same price, so the choice comes down to the setting and your palette rather than cost.

**Q:** Do linen suits wrinkle too much for a wedding?  
**A:** Linen creases by nature, and at an outdoor summer wedding a little wrinkling is expected and reads as part of the fabric&#8217;s character. What matters is whether the wrinkles look intentional or accidental, and that comes down to fit. A suit cut to your body creases along its natural movement lines, which looks relaxed, while a suit that does not fit creases in the excess fabric, which looks careless. A higher-weight linen and a made-to-your-measurements cut is what keeps linen looking considered through a full day.

**Q:** Should the groom and groomsmen wear the same linen color?  
**A:** The groom should sit one or two shades apart from the groomsmen rather than wear an identical color, so he reads as the focal point in every photograph. A common approach is groomsmen in a lighter neutral such as stone or tan with the groom a shade deeper, or the reverse for a beach setting. Because every suit here is made to measure in 18 shared colors, matching the party and separating the groom is straightforward.

**Q:** What is the best linen suit for a summer beach wedding?  
**A:** The best linen suit for a summer beach wedding is a lightweight, breathable 100% linen in a light color like stone, tan, or soft blue, cut half-lined or unlined so it stays cool through the ceremony and reception. The Blue Linen Suit in this collection is built exactly for that, with a half-lined jacket and unlined trousers for genuine airflow. Because it is made to your measurements, it photographs cleanly in bright coastal light rather than pooling and sagging the way a standard-size suit does.

**Q:** Should a groom choose a two-piece or a three-piece linen suit?  
**A:** For a formal ceremony, choose a three-piece, because the waistcoat keeps you formally dressed when the jacket comes off at the reception, which it will at any warm outdoor event. A two-piece is lighter and better suited to a very hot or casual beach ceremony where a jacket may come off for good. Most grooms only realize the value of the waistcoat when they see the reception photographs, where a two-piece leaves them in shirtsleeves and a three-piece does not.

**Q:** How do I make a linen suit formal enough for a wedding?  
**A:** Make a linen suit formal enough for a wedding by choosing a deeper color, adding a waistcoat for a three-piece, and above all getting the fit right. A structured canvas front, a considered color such as navy or stone, and a made-to-your-measurements cut move linen from casual to ceremonial. The single biggest factor is fit: a precisely cut linen suit reads as deliberate and formal, while a loose one reads as a summer holiday jacket no matter the color.

**Q:** Why are good linen wedding suits so hard to find?  
**A:** Good linen wedding suits are hard to find because most are either linen-polyester blends or sold only in standard sizes, and linen shows a poor fit more than any other fabric. A 100% linen suit in the correct weight, cut to your body, solves both problems but is rare on the high street because it cannot be mass produced to a size chart. This collection is made to your measurements in pure linen across 18 colors, which is the combination most buyers are actually looking for.

**Q:** Is 100% linen better than a linen blend for a wedding?  
**A:** 100% linen breathes better and is the more comfortable choice for a hot ceremony, because a blend adds synthetic fibers that trap heat. The trade-off is that a blend, such as the 55% linen and 45% cotton in the Cotton Linen Blazer, creases less and holds a slightly cleaner line over a long day. For genuine heat choose pure linen, and for a suit you want to wear again in more settings a linen-cotton blend is a reasonable middle ground.

**Q:** Can the groom wear a white linen suit?  
**A:** A groom can wear a white linen suit, and it works particularly well at a beach or destination wedding, provided it does not clash with the dress code or compete with the wedding party&#8217;s palette. Off-white, natural, and stone are safer than a pure bright white if you are unsure, and they photograph with a little more warmth in daylight. Because the White Linen Suit is made to your measurements in 18 colors, you can start with white and change your mind without changing the price.

**Q:** Can I order a linen wedding suit for a wedding abroad without an in-person fitting?  
**A:** Yes, the entire process is remote, so you can order for a wedding abroad without visiting us. You enter your measurements on the product page, we ship a free test suit to your home, you try it on and send notes, we correct the pattern, then we cut and ship the final suit to any address you choose. For a destination wedding, order at least 10 to 12 weeks ahead so there is comfortable time for both the test suit and the final production.

**Q:** How long does it take to receive a made-to-your-measurements linen wedding suit?  
**A:** Production takes about four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, including the test-suit stage, and trackable courier delivery adds roughly five to ten business days. For a wedding with a fixed date, tell us the date when you order so we can confirm the timeline honestly. If your date is close, contact us before ordering and we will tell you what is possible rather than guess.
