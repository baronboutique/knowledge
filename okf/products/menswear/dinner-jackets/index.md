## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is the difference between a dinner jacket and a tuxedo?  
**A:** A tuxedo is the complete matching set, a jacket and trousers cut from the same cloth, while a dinner jacket in the modern sense is a statement evening jacket, often velvet, white, or patterned, worn with separate black formal trousers. Both are black-tie garments, but the tuxedo is the classic uniform and the dinner jacket is the way to keep that formality while standing apart from a room of identical tuxedos. Strictly, &#8220;dinner jacket&#8221; is also the British term for the tuxedo jacket itself, which is why the words overlap.

**Q:** When should you wear a white dinner jacket?  
**A:** A white or off-white dinner jacket is worn for warm-weather black tie, traditionally from late spring through summer and at resort, cruise, and destination events. It is worn with black formal trousers, a black bow tie, and a black cummerbund or waistcoat, exactly like a black tuxedo, only with the white jacket in place of the black one. It is the correct seasonal alternative to a black tuxedo and reads as both formal and relaxed in a summer setting.

**Q:** What is a smoking jacket, and can I wear it out?  
**A:** A smoking jacket is a soft, often velvet evening jacket, historically worn at home for a relaxed formal evening, with a shawl collar and sometimes frogging or turned-back cuffs. Today it is worn out as a distinctive black-tie or festive jacket, particularly by hosts, collectors, and men who want the most characterful option in the room. Made to your measurements in velvet, it works as a statement layer at a dinner, a party, or any occasion that rewards a jacket with genuine personality.

**Q:** Is velvet appropriate for black tie?  
**A:** Yes, a velvet dinner jacket is a fully correct and long-established black-tie choice, especially for winter and festive occasions. Velvet catches light with a soft depth that flat wool does not, which is why it reads as considered rather than costume when it is well cut and worn with proper black formal trousers and a bow tie. Deep colors such as burgundy, midnight blue, and black are the most versatile, and all are available here made to your measurements.

**Q:** Can I wear a burgundy or colored dinner jacket to a formal event?  
**A:** Yes, a burgundy or deep-colored dinner jacket is appropriate for most black-tie and formal evening events, provided it is worn with black formal trousers and black-tie accessories. A rich burgundy velvet is the most popular color here precisely because it holds the formality of black tie while making you distinct in a room of black tuxedos. For strict black-tie invitations that specify black only, keep to black or midnight blue.

**Q:** What trousers do you wear with a dinner jacket?  
**A:** A dinner jacket is worn with black formal trousers, ideally with a satin stripe down the outer seam that matches the jacket&#8217;s lapel facing, and no cuff at the hem. The jacket carries the color or texture, and the black trousers keep the outfit anchored in black tie. We can make matching formal trousers to your measurements alongside the jacket so the whole look is coordinated.

**Q:** Should a dinner jacket have a shawl or a peak lapel?  
**A:** Both are correct for a dinner jacket, and the choice is a matter of look. A shawl lapel is the softer, more classic and slightly more relaxed evening collar, and it suits velvet and smoking jackets particularly well. A peak lapel is sharper and the most formal, drawing the eye up and broadening the shoulder. You choose the lapel and its facing, satin or self fabric, as part of your made-to-your-measurements order.

**Q:** Are these dinner jackets made to my measurements or standard sizes?  
**A:** Every dinner jacket here is made to your measurements, with no standard sizes and nothing pre-made. You enter your measurements on the product page, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test jacket so the fit is confirmed on your body first. Fit is what separates a dinner jacket that looks intentional from one that looks borrowed, which matters most on a formal evening.

**Q:** How should a dinner jacket fit?  
**A:** A dinner jacket should fit close through the shoulders and chest, with the shoulder seam sitting at the edge of your shoulder and the jacket long enough to cover the seat. The sleeve should show a little shirt cuff, and the lapel should sit flat against the chest without gaping. Because these are made to your measurements and confirmed with a free test jacket, all of these points are set to your body before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Can I wear a dinner jacket to a wedding?  
**A:** Yes, a dinner jacket is an excellent choice for an evening or black-tie wedding, and increasingly grooms choose a velvet or white dinner jacket to distinguish themselves from guests in standard tuxedos. Worn with black formal trousers, it gives a groom a formal, photograph-ready look with a personal signature, and it is a piece he keeps and wears again to formal events for years.

**Q:** How do I care for a velvet dinner jacket?  
**A:** Dry clean a velvet jacket only when necessary, hang it on a broad wooden hanger to protect the shoulders, and use a soft garment brush in the direction of the pile to lift dust and keep the nap even. Steam rather than press it, holding the steamer just off the surface, so you never crush the pile with a hot iron. Cared for this way, a well-made velvet dinner jacket stays rich and sharp for many years.
