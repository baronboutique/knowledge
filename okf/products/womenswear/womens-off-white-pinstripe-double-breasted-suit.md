Super 130s Merino Wool - What Makes It Work
Super 130s is a fineness grade for worsted wool - the higher the number, the finer the fiber and the smoother the hand. At this grade, the fabric has enough body to hold the double-breasted silhouette and keep the wide-leg trouser falling in a clean, unbroken line. It is fine enough to feel luxurious against the skin and move with the body rather than against it. The off-white pinstripe default is the classic choice - the ground reads ivory rather than white, which is warmer and more flattering under most lighting.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What occasions is a white pinstripe suit appropriate for?  
**A:** This suit works across a wide range of formal and semi-formal occasions: office environments where you want to project authority and individuality, weddings as either the bride or a guest, formal dinners, galas, award ceremonies, film premieres, and networking events. Off-white is versatile - it reads bridal at a wedding, powerful at a boardroom presentation, and editorial at a creative event. It is not appropriate for very casual settings or outdoor summer events where white fabric would be impractical.

**Q:** Can I wear this suit to my wedding instead of a dress?  
**A:** Yes - and more brides are choosing this path. Off-white is the same color family as traditional bridal ivory. The double-breasted silhouette photographs beautifully - the strong shoulder line and wide-leg trouser create long, clean lines that read elegantly in both formal and candid shots. If you want something unconventional but still unmistakably bridal, this suit delivers that. You can request any lining color for a personal detail inside the jacket that only you know about.

**Q:** What is the silhouette of the trousers?  
**A:** Wide-leg with a bell cut - meaning the leg is straight or slightly flared from the knee downward, creating a contemporary, flowing silhouette. This trouser is intentionally fashion-forward rather than traditional. It pairs best with heeled mules, pointed flats, or heeled boots - shoes that give the leg length needed for the wide-leg proportion to work. The flat front and slanted pockets keep the waist clean and smooth. The trouser is cut to your hip, waist, inseam, and rise measurements, so the wide leg falls from your actual proportions rather than a generic block.

**Q:** What does canvas front mean and why does it matter for women's tailoring?  
**A:** A canvas front means the jacket front is structured with a layer of hand-stitched woven interfacing rather than heat-fused glue. In women's tailoring specifically, this makes a meaningful difference: the canvas front molds to the shape of your chest over time, the lapels develop a natural, soft roll that a fused front can never achieve, and the jacket drapes with a fluidity that makes it look more expensive and more fitted. Fused jackets - which most high-street brands use - start stiff and eventually delaminate with dry cleaning. A canvas front lasts the lifetime of the fabric.

**Q:** How do I style a white pinstripe suit without it looking like a costume?  
**A:** Keep everything underneath clean and simple - a fitted black or white bodysuit, a fine-knit top, or a silk camisole. The suit is the statement; the accessories and base layer should support rather than compete. Footwear makes the biggest difference: heeled mules or pointed-toe heels elevate the look immediately. Flat sandals or sneakers work for a deliberately casual take. Avoid anything too matching or too literal - no wide belts with contrasting buckles. The suit reads best when the rest of your outfit steps back.
