## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why do most women's pinstripe suits never fit correctly?  
**A:** Most women's suits, including pinstripe suits, are based on scaled-down versions of men's suit patterns. The shoulder width, trouser rise, and jacket waist suppression are derived from male proportions rather than designed around a woman's body. The result is a jacket that fits somewhere but not everywhere, wide in the back, narrow through the waist, or with a sleeve that falls at the wrong point. A pinstripe makes this worse because the vertical line runs true when the seams are in the right place and crooked when they are not. Building the suit to your specific measurements is the only way to fix this.

**Q:** What is a canvas front and why does it matter in a women's suit?  
**A:** A canvas front is a layer of woven horsehair canvas hand-stitched between the jacket's outer fabric and lining, rather than being glued in place with a fused interlining. In a fused jacket, the front is stiff and holds a fixed shape that may or may not align with your body. In a canvas-front jacket, the canvas floats and gradually molds to your chest and torso over the first few months of wearing. The jacket becomes more yours the more you wear it. Canvas front construction is rare in women's tailoring at any price point. The difference is visible and tactile once you know what to feel for.
