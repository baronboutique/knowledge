The Summer Layer Problem Is Not the Heat. It's the Fit.
Every summer, the same thing happens. You find a linen duster coat that looks right in the photo -- relaxed, long, effortlessly put-together. It arrives and it's three sizes too large through the shoulders, four inches too long in the sleeve, and so shapeless it reads as a robe rather than a coat. You either return it or wear it once. This is not a linen problem. Linen is the right fabric for a summer layer. It is a fit problem -- and off-the-rack dusters are built to a size chart, not a body. Here is the thing about an unlined coat that nobody tells you before you order one: there is nowhere for a bad fit to hide. A lined coat has structure that holds the garment away from the body.

Unlined Means Every Proportion Has to Be Right. The Test Coat Makes Sure It Is.
With a lined coat, the lining adds a layer of structure that smooths over small fit issues. An unlined coat has no such buffer. The way it sits on your shoulders, the way the front hangs open, the way it falls through the torso -- all of it is immediately visible. A shoulder that is half an inch too wide reads as sloppy. A torso that is too long collapses the silhouette. This is why we do not ask you to trust a size chart for this garment. Before we cut your final coat in linen, we make a test coat in cotton and ship it to you first. Try it on over whatever you plan to wear underneath -- a dress, a shirt, jeans. Check the shoulder line.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why does an unlined coat need a test coat more than a lined one?  
**A:** Because there is nothing to hide behind. A lined coat holds its shape independent of the body underneath. An unlined coat drapes directly against whatever it is placed over -- which means every fit issue is immediately visible. A shoulder that is too wide, a torso that is too long, a sleeve that pitches incorrectly -- all of it shows. The test coat lets you see exactly how your coat will fit before we cut the final linen. It is the most important step in the process for this specific garment.

**Q:** Every linen duster coat I've ordered online has been too big. How is this different?  
**A:** Off-the-rack linen dusters are cut to a standardized size chart that tends to run large, particularly through the shoulders and sleeve length. This coat is cut to your measurements -- chest, waist, hips, sleeve length, and total coat length. If the first attempt is not right, the test coat shows you exactly what needs adjusting before the linen is cut. You will not receive a finished coat that does not fit.

**Q:** Can I specify a coat length shorter or longer than mid-thigh?  
**A:** Yes. Note your preferred coat length in the measurement section at checkout. If you want it to hit above the knee, below the knee, or at the knee, specify that and we build to it. The test coat also gives you the opportunity to check the length on your actual frame before the linen is cut -- if it needs adjusting, we adjust before final production.

**Q:** Is linen really machine washable? I thought it needed dry-cleaning.  
**A:** This coat is machine washable. Linen is one of the most practical fabrics for warm weather -- it washes well, dries quickly, and the natural texture means light creasing reads as intentional rather than unkempt. Cold wash, gentle cycle, hang to dry. If it creases in a way you do not like, a light steam press resolves it.

**Q:** Which color works best as a year-round option?  
**A:** Natural linen is the most versatile -- it pairs with almost everything and the slight warmth of the natural fiber reads well in all seasons. Off-white and khaki are similar. If you want maximum contrast impact, black or charcoal works well over light summer fabrics. If you want something distinctive, cornflower and lavender are the two most requested non-neutral options.

**Q:** How does this work as a travel piece?  
**A:** Linen packs flat and is light enough not to add meaningful weight to luggage. It creases in transit but the creases soften quickly when worn or hung in a steamy bathroom. The mid-thigh length means it layers over most outfit combinations you would pack. Made to your measurements, it fits whether you are dressed up for a dinner or dressed down for a long travel day.

**Q:** How long does production take?  
**A:** Allow 3-4 weeks from test coat approval to delivery of your final coat. This includes producing the test coat, shipping it to you, reviewing your feedback, and producing the final linen coat. Both the test coat and the final coat ship worldwide by trackable courier.

**Q:** Do you ship internationally?  
**A:** Yes. We ship worldwide by trackable courier. Both the test coat and the final coat go to your address regardless of location.
