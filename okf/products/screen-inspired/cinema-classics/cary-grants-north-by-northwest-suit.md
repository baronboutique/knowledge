## Questions & Answers

**Q:** I've seen grey glen plaid suits for a fraction of the price. What am I actually paying for here?  
**A:** The pattern and the test suit. A cheap grey glen plaid suit has the pattern. What it does not have is the 3-roll-2 lapel stance, the ventless back, the high-waist pleated trousers, or a canvas chest and a free test suit. Those are the construction decisions that make the suit look the way it looks in the film. Without them, you have a grey plaid suit. With them, you have this suit. The difference is visible from across a room.

**Q:** Why does the 3-roll-2 stance matter and can I see it in the test suit?  
**A:** On a standard 2-button jacket the lapel breaks sharply at the top button, which tightens and closes the chest. On a 3-roll-2, the lapel rolls over the top button in a soft curve, which opens the chest and creates the long, relaxed line you recognise from the film. Yes -- the test suit is built to the same construction, so you can check the roll before the final wool is cut. If it is not right, we adjust.

**Q:** Can the trousers be made flat-front?  
**A:** Yes. The screen inspired version uses forward-facing double pleats at the natural waist. If you prefer flat-front, note it in the order comments at checkout. The high-waist cut stays the same either way -- the trousers still sit where they are supposed to sit.

**Q:** Can I order this as a gift using measurements from a suit my partner already owns?  
**A:** Yes. Submit the measurements marked as ready -- for example, chest 42" (ready). The test suit will show any adjustments needed before the final wool is cut. If you want to skip the test suit for a surprise, send us photos of how each measurement was taken and we will review them for anything that looks off before we start.

**Q:** I'm allergic to wool. Is there anything you can do?  
**A:** Yes. We can extend the lining to the full length of the trouser leg, front and back, at no extra charge. Note it in the comment box on the measurement page at checkout, or email us with your order number.

**Q:** How long will this take?  
**A:** Allow 4-6 weeks from test suit approval to delivery of the final suit. That covers the test suit, shipping it to you, any adjustments, and production of the final garment. Both pieces ship worldwide by trackable courier.

**Q:** The original was made by Kilgour on Savile Row. How does this compare?  
**A:** Kilgour's Arthur Lyons made the principal suits for the film -- he also tailored for the Duke of Windsor. Quintino in Beverly Hills made the stunt duplicates from the same pattern. What every version shared: bespoke construction to Grant's measurements, canvas fronts, and the exact design decisions that define the silhouette. This inspired version uses the same construction method -- canvas fronts, hand-stitched armholes, hand-finished buttonholes -- built to your measurements with a test suit to confirm fit before the final cloth is cut. Different address. Same method.

**Q:** Do you ship internationally?  
**A:** Yes. We ship worldwide by trackable courier. Both the test suit and the final suit go to your address regardless of location.
