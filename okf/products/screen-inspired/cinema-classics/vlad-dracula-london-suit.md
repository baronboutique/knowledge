## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Why do most Gary Oldman Dracula costumes look wrong even when the color is right?  
**A:** Because the color is not what makes this suit recognizable -- the construction is. The inset dupioni silk lapels create a wool-to-silk texture contrast that flat polyester satin cannot replicate. The double-breasted silk vest with peak lapels and collar is a specific garment that most replicas either skip or get wrong. The princess seam frock coat back gives the jacket its shape -- without it, the coat hangs flat and reads as a generic Victorian jacket. Getting the grey right and skipping these details produces a grey coat, not this suit.

**Q:** What is dupioni silk and why does it matter for the lapels and vest?  
**A:** Dupioni is a textured silk woven from irregular threads that produces a distinctive slubbed surface. It catches light differently from smooth silk -- a slight sheen without looking synthetic or shiny. On this suit it creates a deliberate contrast with the matte 140s wool: the lapels and vest read as a different material entirely, which is the visual detail that makes the costume specific. Polyester satin produces a flat, shiny surface that looks wrong against wool under any lighting.
