# Doctor Collection

Tailored Across Time The Doctors have always dressed with purpose. Our Doctor inspired collection brings that same intention to your wardrobe, with every piece custom-made and hand-tailored to your exact measurements. From Tennant's timeless pinstripe suit to Capaldi's commanding velvet frock coat, each garment is crafted with the detail and care it deserves. And to make sure it fits you perfectly, we send a complimentary test garment to your home before we begin the final piece.

**Licensing note:** These garments are inspired by screen productions and are NOT officially licensed unless explicitly stated on the individual product page.

All 7 products made to order. Bespoke orders include a complimentary cotton test garment.

Every Incarnation Dressed With Intent. We Tailor Each One to You.
Few characters in television are defined by their clothes the way the Doctor is. Each incarnation arrives with a wardrobe that says who they are before a word is spoken: the Fourth’s bohemian sprawl, the Seventh’s question-mark whimsy, the Tenth’s sharp pinstripe, the Twelfth’s severe velvet. The problem for anyone who wants to wear one of these looks is that the internet is full of thin, flat costume versions that fall apart after a convention and never quite look right in the first place. This collection is the opposite.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Which Doctor&#8217;s outfits can you make?  
**A:** The collection covers nearly every incarnation. From the classic era: the First Doctor&#8217;s Edwardian black dress coat, the Second Doctor&#8217;s black coat, the Third Doctor&#8217;s green velvet jacket, the Fourth Doctor&#8217;s corduroy and frock coats, the Fifth Doctor&#8217;s cream frock coat, and the Seventh Doctor&#8217;s jacket. From the modern era: the Tenth Doctor&#8217;s blue and brown pinstripe suits, the Eleventh Doctor&#8217;s frock coats and tuxedo, the Twelfth Doctor&#8217;s velvet and Crombie coats, and the Thirteenth Doctor&#8217;s trousers, plus the Master&#8217;s coat for the villain&#8217;s look. Each is made to your exact measurements, and if an incarnation or piece is not listed, we can build it from reference images.

**Q:** Can you make the Tenth Doctor&#8217;s blue pinstripe suit as worn by David Tennant?  
**A:** Yes, this is the most-requested piece in the collection. We make the Tenth Doctor&#8217;s suit in both the blue-with-a-red-stripe and the brown-with-a-blue-stripe versions, cut to your measurements in the correct lightweight suiting cloth so the pinstripe spacing and the slightly relaxed silhouette read accurately. It is built as a genuine, wearable suit, so many customers wear it as a distinctive everyday suit as much as a screen-accurate one. As with every order, a free test garment confirms the fit before we cut the final cloth.

**Q:** What is the difference between the blue and the brown Tenth Doctor suits?  
**A:** They are two distinct suits worn across different stretches of the character&#8217;s run. The brown suit has a blue pinstripe and a slightly warmer, more casual feel, often worn with a tan overcoat. The blue suit has a red or burgundy pinstripe and reads a touch sharper. Both are made to your measurements here, and customers frequently order the version tied to their favorite series, or both. Tell us which you want on the product page.

**Q:** Do you make the Seventh Doctor&#8217;s jacket and the question-mark accessories?  
**A:** Yes. The Seventh Doctor&#8217;s jacket is one of our best-loved classic-era pieces, softly tailored and made to your measurements, and it is designed to pair with the character&#8217;s signature question-mark pullover and hat for the complete look. If you want the full ensemble, note it in your order and we will advise on the matching pieces.

**Q:** Which Twelfth Doctor coats do you make?  
**A:** The Twelfth Doctor range is our deepest for a single incarnation. It includes the maroon and navy velvet coats, the black velvet frock coat, the Series 9 Crombie-style wool coat, the blue coat with red lining, and the grey linen Series 10 coat. Each is made to your measurements in the appropriate cloth, velvet for the frock coats, worsted wool and linen for the overcoats, so you can match a specific series look precisely.

**Q:** Can you make the Fourth Doctor&#8217;s coat as worn by Tom Baker?  
**A:** Yes, in several versions from across the era: the rust and oxblood corduroy coat, the wine velvet frock coat, the beige frock coat, and the darker Horror of Fang Rock coat. Each is made to your measurements in proper cloth. These pieces pair with the character&#8217;s famous long scarf for the complete bohemian silhouette, which we can also supply.

**Q:** Are these screen-accurate replicas or loosely inspired versions?  
**A:** Each piece is made to capture the look and feel of the original as closely as possible, using reference images throughout construction to guide the silhouette, the details, and the cloth. Where an exact original fabric is no longer commercially available, the closest equivalent is sourced. Because these are real tailored garments rather than printed costume pieces, they hold their shape and read accurately both on camera and in person.

**Q:** Are the garments made to my measurements, and is there a fit guarantee?  
**A:** Yes to both. Every piece is made to your measurements with no standard sizes, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test garment in a low-cost fabric so the fit is confirmed on your body first. You try it on at home, check the fit, and send any adjustments, which we apply before cutting the final garment. The test garment is yours to keep. This is how we guarantee fit for customers ordering from anywhere in the world.

**Q:** Can I wear a Doctor inspired suit as everyday clothing?  
**A:** Many of these pieces are built to be worn well beyond a convention. The Tenth Doctor&#8217;s pinstripe suit in particular works as a striking, distinctive everyday suit, and the classic-era coats and jackets read as characterful real-world outerwear. Because they are cut to your measurements in genuine cloth, they wear and last like the tailored garments they are, not like costumes. The more theatrical pieces, such as the velvet frock coats, are equally at home at events, on stage, and in a collector&#8217;s wardrobe.

**Q:** Can you make an incarnation or a specific piece that is not listed?  
**A:** Yes. If you want a look from an incarnation or a specific scene that is not currently listed, contact us with reference images and a description. We accept custom requests for coats, jackets, suits, and accessories, assess the construction, and provide a quote before any work begins. This is often how customers commission a precise series-specific variant.

**Q:** Do you make matching trousers, scarves, and accessories?  
**A:** Yes. Alongside the coats and jackets we make the Thirteenth Doctor&#8217;s trousers and can supply matching trousers for the suits, the Fourth Doctor&#8217;s long scarf, and other accessory pieces to complete a look. Note what you need in your order and we will confirm availability and pricing for the accompanying items.

**Q:** How long does an order take, and do you ship to the UK and worldwide?  
**A:** Production typically takes four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, including the free test-garment stage, and we ship worldwide by FedEx, DHL, or UPS with full tracking, including throughout the UK where these pieces are especially popular. Most orders arrive within a few business days of dispatch. For a convention or event with a fixed date, order with a comfortable margin so there is time for the test garment and any adjustments.
