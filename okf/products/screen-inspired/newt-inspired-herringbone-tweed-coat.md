Authentic British herringbone tweed with dark leather accents
The cloth is a wool-rich herringbone tweed, woven in Britain. Herringbone is a structured weave: the V-shaped pattern you see is built into the fabric at the loom, which means it does not pill or shift the way printed fabrics do, and it gives the coat a depth and texture that reads differently in daylight than it does indoors. In overcoat weight, herringbone has been the signature of serious British outerwear for over a century. It is the cloth a real 1920s Edwardian naturalist would have worn. The dark leather accents on the pocket flaps are genuine leather, hand-stitched into position. They are not vinyl trim or bonded leather piping.

**Q:** Is this coat inspired by Newt Scamander's screen coat?  
**A:** Yes. The fabric and leather details are our own interpretation of the Edwardian British adventurer aesthetic the screen wardrobe draws from.

**Q:** Why herringbone tweed instead of the blue-green wool from the film?  
**A:** The screen coat is a distinctive teal blue wool. We chose British herringbone tweed for our version because it is truer to the Edwardian British outerwear tradition the character's wardrobe references, and because it wears as serious everyday outerwear rather than a theatrical piece. Herringbone tweed in overcoat weight is a garment you can wear to dinner, to work, or to a convention. The teal screen color reads as costume outside a specific context.
