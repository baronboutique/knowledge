W. Bill Shetland tweed from Harrisons of Edinburgh
The cloth is pure new wool, 390gsm, from the W. Bill Shetland Tweeds bunch by Harrisons of Edinburgh. The same English merchant house that has supplied Savile Row tailors and British costume departments for over 160 years. It is the cloth itself, not a "tweed-style" alternative, not a "Shetland-look" wool blend. Why this matters. Shetland tweed is woven from undyed yarns of multiple natural shades, which is why a single jacket reads as a different color in afternoon sun than it does indoors at a convention. It photographs like a piece of British countryside. It softens and personalizes with every wear. And it lasts, in a way the polyester jackets sold elsewhere on the internet do not.
