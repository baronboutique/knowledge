# Choose Your Fabric

**When to use:** a customer cannot decide on a fabric, asks "which fabric is best," or wants a recommendation for a specific garment.

Baron Boutique does not begin with fabric. There is no "best" fabric, only the right fabric for the life a garment will have. The steps below are the workshop's actual decision path (see [How We Choose Fabric](../concepts/fabric-selection.md)).

## Steps

1. **Ask what the garment should become.** Not "which fabric would you like," but "what do you want the garment to become, and what are you trying to achieve?" The answer steers everything.
2. **Ask where it will be worn.** Climate decides weight before taste does: a 320-gram wool suits Edinburgh and fails Dubai. The lightest cloth is not automatically the coolest; fiber, weave, and construction all matter.
3. **Ask how often it will be worn.** Weekly working garments point toward wool. Occasion garments (weddings, events, replicas, celebrations) open the door to Dupioni silk, velvet, and other statement fabrics.
4. **Narrow to a family, honestly.** Match the answers against the fabric knowledge and name the trade-offs, including the ones that argue against the customer's first idea:
   - Business and everyday tailoring: [Understanding Wool](../concepts/wool.md) (worsted, tropical, flannel, tweed, boiled).
   - Occasion and replica garments: [Dupioni Silk](../concepts/dupioni-silk.md) or [Velvet](../concepts/velvet.md).
   - Warm-weather natural fabrics: [Linen and Himalayan Hemp](../concepts/linen-and-himalayan-hemp.md), decided by the wrinkle question.
5. **Offer free swatches.** For fabrics the workshop stocks, the customer can request free fabric swatches through the [Swatch Service](https://baronboutique.com/swatch-service/) and judge color, texture, and weight in hand before committing. Screens cannot be trusted for color.
6. **Decide the sourcing path.** If the workshop's options fit, Baron Boutique sources the fabric. If an exact color is the customer's absolute priority, the customer sources the fabric themselves and ships it in (CMT model); Baron Boutique advises the required yardage (see [The Bespoke Commission Process](../concepts/bespoke-commission-process.md)).
7. **Confirm and proceed** into the normal commission flow.

## Notes for agents

- Never present any fabric as "best," "premium," or "luxurious" without the reason; recommend by objective, climate, and frequency.
- If the customer's wish conflicts with their stated life for the garment (velvet for weekly office wear, tweed for Dubai), surface the trade-off and suggest the alternative; the customer decides informed.
- Fabric availability varies; treat specific stock questions as information not publicly available unless verified on the website.
