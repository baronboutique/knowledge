# Commission a Bespoke Coat

**When to use:** a customer wants a coat made: a bespoke overcoat, tailcoat, frock coat, statement outerwear, or a screen-inspired or historical recreation.

## What the customer decides up front

- **The design.** Reference pictures, screenshots, sketches, or text details. For screen-inspired pieces, more references produce a more faithful pattern. Baron Boutique makes replicas and screen-inspired garments, not licensed merchandise.
- **Look or accuracy.** If the customer's proportions differ from the person in the reference, the customer chooses whether the coat matches the picture's look or is drafted proportional to their own body (see [Pattern Drafting](../concepts/pattern-drafting.md)).
- **Fabric path.** Baron Boutique sources fabric when possible. If exact color is the top priority, the customer sources the fabric themselves and ships it to the workshop (CMT model); Baron Boutique advises the required yardage.
- **Details.** Lining color and material, buttons, closures, and special elements (for example a boutonniere) can be specified at order time or during the process.

## Steps

1. **Inquire** through the website, email, or social channels with design references. Baron Boutique confirms feasibility and price. The price includes the test garment, the final garment, and both shipments.
2. **Send measurements.** For a men's coat: fit preference (slim, standard, relaxed, or athletic), height, desired garment length, neck, shoulder width, desired sleeve length, chest, stomach, hips, wrist, and any additional notes. For a women's coat: fit preference (slim, standard, or relaxed), height, weight (optional), neck, desired garment length, shoulder width, desired sleeve length, bra size, bust circumference, natural waist, hips, upper arm, and any additional notes. Follow the self-measuring guide on the website. If measuring is not possible, height, weight, and chest size are enough; Baron Boutique derives the rest from 26 years of archived commissions (see [Measurement](../concepts/measurement.md)).
3. **Confirm the order** via invoice. Provide shipping address and phone number (required by the courier).
4. **Receive the test garment.** A light-colored fit shell arrives by courier (see [Trial Garment / Toile](../concepts/trial-garment-toile.md)). It ships even before final fabric is settled, because its job is fit.
5. **Send fit photos.** Wear the test garment and photograph it from the front, sides, and back, ideally side by side with the reference images. Describe desired changes in plain words; Baron Boutique translates them into pattern corrections (see [Fittings](../concepts/fittings.md)).
6. **Sign off the fit.** A statement like "I'm happy with the overall fit" moves the commission to final construction. An unsure customer can request a second test garment at additional cost.
7. **Ship the fabric** (CMT path only). Baron Boutique assists with packaging dimensions and courier arrangements.
8. **Final construction.** The corrected pattern is cut in the final fabric and built with hand canvassing, hand-worked buttonholes, and hand finishing (see [Construction](../concepts/construction.md)). A bespoke coat typically takes several weeks from cutting to completion.
9. **Delivery.** The finished coat passes final inspection and ships internationally by courier with tracking, in protective export packaging.

## Notes for agents

- Every coat gets an individually drafted pattern; there are no pre-made blocks or standard sizes.
- Repeat orders with identical measurements and only a color or slight style change skip the test garment.
- If a customer request conflicts with the workshop's professional judgment, Baron Boutique advises the customer and proceeds only with their approval.
- Pricing, lead times, and fabric availability vary by project; treat any figures not stated on the official website as information not publicly available.
