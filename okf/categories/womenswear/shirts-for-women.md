## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Do you make tailored dress shirts for women?  
**A:** Yes. Every shirt is cut to your exact measurements in real cloth, with no standard sizing, so it fits cleanly across the shoulders, bust, and waist, which is exactly where ready-made shirts fail. You choose the cloth, collar, cuff, and fit, so the shirt suits both the boardroom and everyday wear.

**Q:** Can I customize the collar, cuff, and fit?  
**A:** Yes. Because each shirt is made to order, you choose the collar style, the cuff, and how closely fitted or relaxed you want it. Tell us on the product page, and the free test garment stage lets you confirm the fit on your body before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Do you make silk blouses?  
**A:** Yes. We offer a Crepe Silk Blouse and a Tie-Waist Crepe Silk shirt dress, both made to your measurements in fluid crepe silk. Because they are tailored to you, they drape cleanly without the gaping or pulling that off-the-rack silk blouses often show.

**Q:** Which fabrics can I choose?  
**A:** Egyptian cotton, oxford cotton, twill, linen for relaxed and oversized styles, and crepe silk for blouses, in a range of colors. Each product page lists the cloths available, and we can advise on the best choice for your climate and how you plan to wear the shirt.

**Q:** How does the fit work, and is there a guarantee?  
**A:** Each shirt is made to your measurements with no standard sizes, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test garment so the fit is confirmed on your body. You try it on at home, check the shoulders, bust, and sleeve, and send any adjustments, which we apply before cutting the final shirt. The test garment is yours to keep.

**Q:** How long does it take, and do you ship worldwide?  
**A:** Production typically takes four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, including the free test-garment stage, and we ship worldwide by FedEx, DHL, or UPS with full tracking. For a dated event, order with a comfortable margin so there is time for the test garment and any adjustments.
