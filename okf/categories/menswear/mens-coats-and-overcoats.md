An Overcoat Off the Rack Fails on the Two Things That Matter: the Shoulders and the Length.
A coat is the hardest-working thing in a wardrobe. You wear it over everything, in every photograph taken between October and March, for years. And it is the garment an off-the-rack size gets most wrong, because the two things that make a coat look right, the set of the shoulders and the length of the hem, are the two things a rack size cannot fix. A coat that ends too short reads as an afterthought no matter how good the fabric is, and the modern trend toward short, skinny coats has made this worse, not better.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is the difference between an overcoat, a topcoat, a peacoat, and a car coat?  
**A:** The difference is length, weight, and formality. An overcoat is the longest and warmest, falling at or below the knee in heavy wool for cold weather. A topcoat is the lighter, slightly shorter version for milder weather, also worn over a suit. A peacoat is hip-length and double-breasted with a broad collar, a nautical look that is warmer and more casual than an overcoat. A car coat is the shortest, ending around the seat, cut that way so it would not bunch when driving, and it is the most versatile of the four.

**Q:** How long should a men&#8217;s overcoat be?  
**A:** A men&#8217;s overcoat should fall at or just below the knee, which is long enough to read as a proper coat and to cover a suit jacket completely. The modern trend toward short, skinny overcoats that end at mid-thigh looks dated quickly and leaves the jacket hanging out below the hem. Because our coats are made to your measurements, you set the exact length on your body during the test-coat stage, which matters especially if you are taller or shorter than average.

**Q:** What is a car coat and how long is it?  
**A:** A car coat is a shorter overcoat that ends at or just below the seat, roughly mid-thigh, with a relaxed A-line cut. It got its name because the shorter length made it comfortable to wear while driving, unlike a full-length overcoat. That length is exactly why it is so versatile: it works over a suit, over a sweater, or over jeans, and reads as smart without being formal. Baron&#8217;s car coats are made to your measurements in tweed and herringbone wool.

**Q:** What is the difference between a peacoat and an overcoat?  
**A:** A peacoat is hip-length, double-breasted, and cut from heavy wool with a broad collar, originally naval, while an overcoat is longer, falling at or below the knee, and usually more formal. An overcoat is warmer over the legs and better over a suit for the office or an evening, while a peacoat is shorter, sharper, and more casual. The choice comes down to how long and how formal you want the coat to be, and both are available here made to your measurements.

**Q:** Why does an off-the-rack coat never seem to fit right?  
**A:** Because a coat&#8217;s fit lives in the shoulders and the length, and those are the two things a standard size cannot adjust to your body. A rack coat is cut to an average shoulder width and an average height, so on most men the shoulder sits wrong or the hem lands short, and neither can be fixed by a tailor after the fact without recutting the coat. A coat made to your measurements sets the shoulder and the length to you from the start, which is the difference between a coat that looks bought and one that looks made.

**Q:** Does a canvas front matter in a coat, or only in a suit?  
**A:** It matters in a coat too. A canvas front is a layer of woven interlining stitched into the chest that lets the coat hold its shape and mold to your body over time. The cheaper alternative, a fused front, is glued, and after a season or two of cold and cleaning it bubbles and the chest collapses. For a coat you plan to wear for years, canvas front construction is what keeps it looking sharp, and it is how Baron builds the coats that call for structure.

**Q:** What is a frock coat?  
**A:** A frock coat is a knee-length tailored coat with a defined waist seam and a flared skirt below it, the primary formal coat for men in the 19th century and still the most dramatic silhouette available today. It is more formal and more theatrical than a modern overcoat, which is why it is chosen for weddings, ceremonies, and by collectors. Baron makes frock coats to your measurements in wool, velvet, and linen, and it is one of the looks the workshop is best known for.

**Q:** What fabric is warmest for a winter coat?  
**A:** Melton wool is the warmest common overcoat fabric, a dense, tightly woven wool that blocks wind and holds heat, followed by heavy wool-cashmere blends that add softness and a little more warmth for the weight. Tweed and herringbone are warm and hard-wearing and are the classic choices for car coats and country coats. For the coldest climates choose Melton wool or a wool-cashmere overcoat at a full knee length, all of which are available here made to your measurements.

**Q:** Will the coat fit over a suit jacket?  
**A:** Yes, and this is one of the main reasons the free test coat exists. A coat worn over a suit needs a little more room through the chest and shoulders than a coat worn over a shirt, and we build that ease in based on what you tell us you will wear underneath. When your test coat arrives, try it on over your suit and note anything that needs adjusting, and we correct it before cutting the final coat so it layers cleanly.

**Q:** Are these coats made to my measurements or standard sizes?  
**A:** Every coat here is made to your measurements, with no standard sizes and nothing pre-made. You enter your measurements on the product page, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test coat so the fit, the shoulders, and the length are confirmed on your body first. This is the step that lets us get the two hardest parts of a coat, the shoulder and the hem, exactly right.

**Q:** Can I get a coat in a custom length if I am shorter or taller than average?  
**A:** Yes, because the coat is cut to your measurements, the length is set to your height rather than to a size chart. Shorter men can specify a hem that hits at the right point on the knee instead of swallowing the leg, and taller men can get a genuinely full length instead of a coat that ends short. You confirm the exact length on your body during the free test-coat stage before the final coat is cut.

**Q:** Do you make screen-inspired and statement coats as well as classics?  
**A:** Yes, alongside overcoats, peacoats, and car coats, Baron makes the coats people recognize from the screen, including the 12th Doctor&#8217;s velvet frock coats, the Matrix and Neo trench silhouettes, the Fantastic Beasts Graves coat, and more. Each is made to your measurements in real wool, velvet, or leather so it wears as genuine outerwear rather than a costume. You can explore these in the Screen Inspired collections linked above.

**Q:** How do I care for a wool coat, and do you ship worldwide?  
**A:** Dry clean a wool coat only when it needs it, hang it on a broad wooden hanger to keep the shoulders, and brush it down between wears to keep the wool clean, which will keep a well-made coat looking sharp for many years. We ship worldwide by trackable courier from Dubai, both the free test coat and the final coat, and you receive tracking as soon as each package ships.
