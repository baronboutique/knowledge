## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Should I rent or buy a tuxedo?  
**A:** Buy, unless you genuinely expect to wear black tie only once in your life. A rental is fitted in minutes to whatever is roughly your size, so it rarely fits well and the fabric is worn from previous renters. A made-to-your-measurements tuxedo costs about what two or three rentals cost, fits your body correctly, and is yours to keep, which makes buying the better value for anyone who attends a formal event more than a couple of times.

**Q:** How much does a custom tuxedo cost compared to renting?  
**A:** A rental typically runs $100 to $200 each time, while a tuxedo in this collection starts at $599 and is yours permanently. That means a bought tuxedo pays for itself in roughly two to three formal events, and every event after that costs nothing. Over a decade of weddings, galas, and black-tie evenings, buying is far cheaper than repeatedly renting.

**Q:** How should a tuxedo fit?  
**A:** A tuxedo should fit close through the shoulders and chest, with the jacket long enough to cover the seat of your trousers and sleeves that show a little shirt cuff. The trousers should have a clean break at the shoe with no pooling, and no cuff at the hem. Fit is the single biggest reason a tuxedo looks expensive or cheap, which is why every tuxedo here is made to your measurements and confirmed with a free test suit before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Should tuxedo trousers have cuffs?  
**A:** No, tuxedo trousers should never have cuffs. They finish with a plain hem and carry a satin stripe down the outer seam that matches the lapel facing. Cuffs belong on day trousers, not on formal evening wear, and are one of the most common tells of a rental or an off-the-rack tuxedo.

**Q:** Do I wear a bow tie or a necktie with a tuxedo?  
**A:** A tuxedo is worn with a bow tie. A straight necktie against a black tuxedo creates a hard vertical line down the chest and reads as less formal, which is why traditional black tie calls for a bow tie, ideally self-tie in black silk or satin to match the lapel facing.

**Q:** What color tuxedo should I choose?  
**A:** Black is the most formal and the safest choice, and midnight blue or navy reads even richer under evening light because true black can absorb venue lighting and look flat. Burgundy is the standout for a man who wants color and intends to be the best-dressed person in the room. All three are available here, each made to your measurements.

**Q:** Is a peak lapel, shawl lapel, or notch lapel best?  
**A:** A peak lapel is the most formal and the most flattering, because the upward points broaden the shoulders and draw the eye up. A shawl lapel is the classic, softer, and slightly more relaxed evening look. A notch lapel is the most versatile and least formal, and works well on a first tuxedo or a dinner jacket meant to double for other occasions.

**Q:** Is navy or black more formal for black tie?  
**A:** Black is the traditional black-tie standard, but a midnight-blue or navy tuxedo is fully acceptable at almost any black-tie event and often photographs richer. This is because true black can read as a flat, featureless shape under venue lighting, while a deep navy or midnight blue holds its depth and shows the cut of the garment. If the invitation says black tie, both black and midnight blue are correct.

**Q:** Can I wear a tuxedo to a wedding?  
**A:** Yes, a tuxedo is appropriate for an evening or formal wedding, and grooms increasingly choose one to distinguish themselves from guests wearing ordinary suits. A made-to-your-measurements tuxedo in black, midnight blue, or burgundy gives a groom a formal, photograph-ready look that a rental cannot match, and it becomes a piece he keeps and wears again for years.

**Q:** Are these tuxedos made to my measurements or standard sizes?  
**A:** Every tuxedo here is made to your measurements, with no standard sizes and nothing pre-made. You enter your measurements on the product page, and before we cut your final cloth we send a free test suit so the fit is confirmed on your body first. This is the step that separates a tuxedo that fits from a rental that is close but never right.

**Q:** How long does a bought tuxedo last?  
**A:** A well-made tuxedo built on a canvas front lasts decades, and black-tie style barely changes, so it does not date the way fashion suiting can. The satin lapels, the silhouette, and the color conventions of black tie have held for generations, which means a tuxedo bought correctly today will still be correct in twenty years. The ample seam allowances built into each one also allow for future alteration as your fit changes.

**Q:** How far in advance should I order for an event?  
**A:** Order at least 8 to 10 weeks before the event so there is comfortable time for the test suit and any adjustments before the final tuxedo is made and shipped. Production takes about four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, including the test-suit stage. If your event is sooner, contact us before ordering and we will tell you honestly whether the timeline works.
