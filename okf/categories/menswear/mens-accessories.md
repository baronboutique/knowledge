## Questions & Answers

**Q:** Are these ties and scarves real silk, or polyester?  
**A:** Real silk. The neckties, pocket squares, tie-and-square sets, and the aviator neck scarf are all 100% satin silk, never polyester or a blend. Most accessories at this price online are polyester woven to imitate the sheen, which reads synthetic and slick in person. Genuine satin silk has a softer, warmer luster, holds a knot and a fold better, and lasts far longer. The cashmere scarves and pashmina shawl are likewise 100% Grade A cashmere, not blended.

**Q:** How do I match a tie and pocket square to my suit?  
**A:** The simplest reliable approach is to let the tie relate to the suit and the pocket square relate to the shirt or add a contrast, rather than matching the tie and square to each other exactly. A tie-and-pocket-square set cut from the same silk gives you a guaranteed match if you prefer that, while separate pieces let you build a more layered look. With over 20 tie colors and 21 square colors available, you can coordinate precisely, from a tonal complement to a deliberate contrast.

**Q:** Can I get a tie in a custom length or width?  
**A:** Yes. The neckties come in skinny, standard, and extra-large widths to match your lapel and build, and the length can be adjusted at no extra cost, which matters for taller or shorter wearers who find standard ties finish in the wrong place. Custom silk colors are also available on request. Add your requirements in the order notes or contact us before ordering, and we will confirm.

**Q:** What size is the pocket square, and how do I fold it?  
**A:** The silk pocket squares are 17 inches square, which is a generous size that holds a fold without slipping down into the pocket over an evening. That size suits the crisp, flat presidential fold, the classic two- or three-point fold, and the relaxed puff. A smaller square tends to disappear into the pocket; the 17-inch size gives you enough material to shape whichever fold you prefer and keep it in place.

**Q:** How do I care for silk and cashmere accessories?  
**A:** Silk ties are best kept rolled or hung and dry cleaned when needed; avoid over-tightening the knot and untie rather than pull it apart to protect the shape. Silk pocket squares and scarves can be dry cleaned or gently hand washed in cool water with a mild silk-friendly detergent, then air dried in the shade and ironed on low. Cashmere scarves are hand washed in cool water with a wool-and-cashmere shampoo, dried flat, and stored folded with cedar or lavender to deter moths. Cared for this way, both last for years.

**Q:** Can accessories be personalized or monogrammed for a gift?  
**A:** Yes. Cashmere scarves, wraps, and the pashmina shawl can be monogrammed or personalized with initials, and ties can be cut to a custom length and width, which turns any of them into a genuinely individual gift rather than an off-the-shelf one. Because they are real silk and cashmere at a recognizable quality level, they read as a considered present. See the cashmere collection for personalization options.

**Q:** What makes a good accessory gift for a man?  
**A:** Accessories are among the best practical-luxury gifts because they need no sizing and suit almost anyone. A silk pocket square at $65 or a tie is an accessible entry point; a tie-and-square set or a men&#8217;s cashmere scarf sits in the mid range; and the pashmina shawl or a personalized cashmere piece is a standout gift. The wide color range and the option to monogram let you choose something specific to the recipient rather than defaulting to a safe neutral.

**Q:** Do you ship worldwide, and how long does it take?  
**A:** Yes, we ship worldwide from Dubai by trackable courier. Ready-made ties, squares, and scarves dispatch quickly, usually within a few business days. Personalized or monogrammed pieces and made-to-order items take a little longer because they are finished specifically for you, with the timeline confirmed at checkout. For a gift with a deadline, order with a comfortable margin or contact us and we will confirm what is achievable.
