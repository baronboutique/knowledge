Screen Inspired Garments Made to Your Measurements — Trench Coats, Doctor Inspired, Spy Wear & More
Every piece in this collection is built from scratch to capture an iconic screen look — the signature leather trench coat, the Doctor inspired frock coat, the classic spy suit, the Victorian Ulster coat. Unlike off-the-rack costume pieces, each garment is cut to your exact measurements with fabric chosen to closely reflect the original: wool blends, fine suiting cloth, brushed cashmere, or structured outerwear fabric depending on the style. Every order includes a complimentary test garment before the final piece is cut, so you can confirm the fit before committing. Ships worldwide via FedEx, DHL, or UPS with full tracking.

## Questions & Answers

**Q:** How does the bespoke process work for a screen inspired coat or suit?  
**A:** You share your measurements and choose the character piece you want. Baron Boutique cuts a free test coat first — a muslin or low-cost fabric version of the garment — and ships it to you before the final piece is made. You try it on, note any fit adjustments, and return the feedback. The final piece is then cut in the correct fabric. This two-stage process means the finished garment fits your body, not a size chart.

**Q:** Can you make the iconic long black trench coat — the one worn by Neo?  
**A:** Yes. The Neo inspired long trench coat is one of the most requested pieces in this collection. It is made in a medium-weight structured cotton with the correct floor-length silhouette, wide lapels, and deep back vent. The white long coat version as seen in the sequel is also available. Both are made to your exact measurements.

**Q:** Which Doctor inspired coats and jackets are available?  
**A:** The collection covers multiple Doctor inspired styles: the long bohemian coat as worn by the Fourth Doctor, the Edwardian frock coat as worn by the First Doctor, the black coat as worn by the Second Doctor, the jacket as worn by the Seventh Doctor, the blue and brown pinstripe suits as worn by Tennant, and the navy velvet coat as worn by Capaldi. Each is made to your measurements and can be adjusted for lining, button choice, and fit.

**Q:** What spy inspired suits and outfits can you make?  
**A:** The spy inspired range covers a wide selection — the classic three-piece pinstripe suit, the naval peacoat, the silk dinner jacket, the suede blouson jacket, and several more. That is just a fraction of what can be made. Any iconic spy inspired look is available. Send a reference photo and the team will build it to your exact measurements.

**Q:** What fabrics are used for screen inspired coats compared to the originals?  
**A:** Fabric selection depends on the style. The signature floor-length trench coat uses a medium-weight structured cotton. Doctor inspired frock coats are typically made in fine wool suiting or velvet depending on the style. Classic spy suits are built in Super 100s or 110s wool. For each piece, the fabric is chosen to match the weight, drape, and texture as closely as possible to what is seen on screen.

**Q:** Are these screen inspired garments or just loosely inspired-by versions?  
**A:** Every piece is made to capture the look and feel of the original as closely as possible, given fabric availability and fit requirements. Reference images are used throughout the construction process to guide the silhouette, details, and fabric choice. Where exact fabrics are no longer commercially available, the closest equivalent is sourced. The goal is a wearable garment that looks right on screen and in person, not a loose interpretation.

**Q:** Can you make a screen inspired coat or suit not already listed?  
**A:** Yes. If you have a specific screen inspired piece in mind that is not listed, contact the team with reference images and a description. Custom requests are accepted for coats, jackets, suits, and capes. The team will assess the construction requirements and provide a quote before any work begins.

**Q:** How long does an order take, and where do you ship?  
**A:** Production typically takes four to six weeks from measurement confirmation to dispatch, which includes the free test coat stage. Shipping is worldwide. Customers in the US, UK, Australia, Canada, and Europe receive orders regularly. Shipping timelines vary by destination but most orders arrive within 4-6 business days of dispatch.
