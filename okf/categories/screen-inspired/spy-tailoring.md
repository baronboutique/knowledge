## Questions & Answers

**Q:** How long will a bespoke spy suit last compared to an off-the-rack costume suit?  
**A:** A well-maintained bespoke wool suit lasts 10 to 20 years with proper rotation and storage. Because each suit here is cut from proper suiting fabric — wool, linen, corduroy, or silk — and constructed with a full internal structure, it holds its shape over time the way a tailored suit should. Off-the-rack costume suits, typically made from synthetic blends with minimal internal construction, begin to lose their shape within a year of regular wear.

**Q:** What measurements do I need for a made-to-measure suit?  
**A:** The most critical measurements for a suit are chest, shoulder width, jacket length, waist, seat, trouser inseam, and trouser outseam. A full measurement guide is provided with every order. For the coats in the collection, sleeve length and back length are also required. The free test garment stage exists precisely because measurements alone cannot account for every variation in body shape — the test garment confirms everything before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Is this made to my measurements or are there standard sizes to choose from?  
**A:** Every piece is made to your measurements. There are no standard sizes in this collection. You submit your measurements, a test garment is cut to those measurements and shipped to you for fit confirmation, and the final suit is then made to the adjusted pattern. This is a bespoke process, not a size-selection process.

**Q:** Which James Bond suit is the most versatile for everyday wear?  
**A:** The Skyfall charcoal grey pinstripe is the most wearable piece in the collection for everyday use. It is a slim two-piece in a neutral color that reads as a sharp business suit without the three-piece formality of the Casino Royale. The Skyfall grey sharkskin is a close second for buyers who want a slightly more distinctive fabric. The linen suit from The World Is Not Enough is the best choice for warm climates.

**Q:** What is the difference between the Casino Royale suit and the Skyfall suits?  
**A:** The Casino Royale suit is a navy pinstripe three-piece — jacket, trousers, and vest — cut in a more formal silhouette suited to occasions that call for the full Bond treatment. The Skyfall suits are two-piece and cut slimmer and darker. The charcoal pinstripe and grey sharkskin Skyfall suits are closer to modern business suiting and easier to wear in everyday contexts without reading as a costume reference.

**Q:** Can a suit be a spy outfit for cosplay or a themed event?  
**A:** Yes, and a bespoke suit is the most effective version of that look. A suit that fits your body correctly reads entirely differently from a costume suit — the silhouette is sharper, the shoulders sit properly, and the jacket length is right for your proportions. Every piece in this collection is built to the same standard as a tailored commission, which means it works as a wearable suit as well as a screen-accurate cosplay piece. For themed events, the Casino Royale three-piece or the Dr. No Nehru jacket are the strongest immediate visual references.

**Q:** What type of suit does James Bond wear in Casino Royale?  
**A:** In the final scene of Casino Royale, James Bond wears a navy three-piece pinstripe suit — jacket, matching vest, and flat-front trousers — in a fitted silhouette that became one of the most referenced Bond looks of the Daniel Craig era. The suit is cut with a suppressed waist and relatively narrow lapels, which defined the Craig-era Bond aesthetic going forward into Quantum of Solace and Skyfall.

**Q:** Do I need to know my fabric before ordering, or is that decided for me?  
**A:** For screen-inspired pieces, the fabric is selected to match the original as closely as possible — you do not need to specify it. The Casino Royale suit uses a fine pinstripe wool, the Skyfall sharkskin uses a textured wool blend, the World Is Not Enough suit uses 100% linen, and so on. If you have a preference or want to discuss fabric options, the team can work with you on that before the order is confirmed. Custom fabric requests outside the screen-inspired range are also accepted.

**Q:** Why order from Baron Boutique rather than a local tailor for a Bond suit?  
**A:** Baron Boutique has built and shipped these specific pieces hundreds of times. A local tailor can build a suit to your measurements, but replicating a specific screen look requires reference work, fabric sourcing, and construction knowledge specific to each garment. The free test garment process also removes the risk that a local tailor cannot offer on a first commission — you see the fit and silhouette before any final fabric is cut. Every suit ships worldwide with full tracking and a documented alteration process if anything needs adjusting on arrival.
