## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What is the difference between the Melton wool coat and the leather version?  
**A:** The Melton wool coat is the screen inspired choice — the original was made in wool, which is a dense, matte fabric with no synthetic sheen. It drapes with weight and reads as a real coat rather than a costume under convention lighting or in photographs. The leather version uses full-grain cow Nappa and is for buyers who want the same silhouette built to last decades. Leather holds the wide peak lapels and flared cuffs without internal support and does not require dry cleaning. Both are made to your measurements. Both include a free test garment before the final cloth is cut.

**Q:** Why does the test garment matter for these coats specifically?  
**A:** The flared cuffs and the wide peak lapels on these coats are what make the silhouette recognizable. If the sleeve length is off even slightly, the cuffs sit too high or pool at the hand and the entire look changes. The same applies to the lapel roll and the coat length. The test garment is built in cotton to your exact measurements and shipped before any final cloth is cut. You try it on, check every detail, and send your notes. We apply every correction before building the final piece. This is how a screen-accurate silhouette gets made correctly for a real body.

**Q:** Can I order the purple overcoat in a different color?  
**A:** Yes. The Melton wool coat is available in 15 colors including black, charcoal, dark grey, navy, cobalt, teal, burgundy, camel, aubergine, and gold yellow — all at the same price. Purple is the screen-accurate default. The other colors work well for wearing the coat as statement outerwear where you want the silhouette without the direct film reference. The leather coat is available in multiple leather types and colors as well.

**Q:** What is the difference between the overcoat and the tailcoat?  
**A:** The overcoat is the long mid-calf length single-breasted coat with triple rear vents and flared cuffs — the most widely recognized version. The tailcoat is a separate, rarer style: a formal evening piece with theatrical tails, double-breasted look, and tuxedo-inspired construction. Most purple villain pieces on the market are the overcoat. The tailcoat is the dramatic formal alternative. Both are available. Both include a free test garment.

**Q:** Can I wear these pieces outside of conventions?  
**A:** Yes, and many clients do. In black, charcoal, or navy Melton wool the long coat reads as dramatic statement outerwear in editorial, theatrical, and formal contexts with no film reference required. In purple it is unmistakably specific — which is its own statement. The construction quality, canvas front, and seam allowances hold up across years of wear. The burgundy three-piece suit works as formalwear without modification. These are collector-quality garments built to last, not seasonal accessories.

**Q:** Can you make a villain-inspired piece not already listed?  
**A:** Yes. If you have a specific screen-inspired villain garment in mind that is not listed, contact the team with reference images and a description. Custom requests are accepted for coats, suits, tailcoats, and capes. The team will assess the construction requirements and provide a quote before any work begins.

**Q:** How long does an order take, and where do you ship?  
**A:** Allow 6 to 9 weeks from order to final garment. The test garment takes 2 to 3 weeks to build and ship. Once you confirm the fit, the final piece takes 4 to 6 weeks in production. Shipping is worldwide. Customers in the US, UK, Australia, Canada, and Europe receive orders regularly. Both the test garment and the final piece ship via trackable courier.
