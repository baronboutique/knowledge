## Questions & Answers

**Q:** What type of coat does Neo wear in The Matrix?  
**A:** Neo wears a floor-length black wool trench coat with a wide notch lapel, deep back vent, and structured shoulder — designed to read as a modern interpretation of a cassock or ecclesiastical robe. In the original Matrix film the coat is relatively softly structured. In The Matrix Reloaded, the coat becomes more dramatic — longer, with a mandarin collar, no visible buttons, and a flare that begins below the knee rather than the hip. Each film features a slightly different construction, which is why this collection offers a separate coat for each version.

**Q:** What is the difference between the Matrix 1, Reloaded, and Revolutions coats?  
**A:** The Matrix 1 coat has a wide notch lapel, soft structure, and ankle length — the most classic trench coat silhouette of the three. The Matrix Reloaded coat is the most distinctive: mandarin collar, hidden button placket, and a below-ankle length with a flare that creates the movement effect in the highway scene. The Matrix Revolutions coat is closer to the Reloaded version in silhouette but built for more everyday wearability. Baron makes all three to your exact measurements in fabric matched as closely as possible to the original screen version.

**Q:** How long does a bespoke matrix trench coat last with regular wear?  
**A:** A well-constructed wool matrix trench coat worn regularly will last 10 to 15 years with basic care. The limiting factor on any floor-length structured coat is the lining — cheap linings split at the armhole and hem within a few years. Baron coats use a full satin lining with reinforced stress points, which is why clients wear them for a decade without the interior failing. The leather version, with proper conditioning, holds its shape and finish for considerably longer.

**Q:** Can the Matrix coat be worn as everyday outerwear or only for cosplay?  
**A:** Both — and this is the practical difference between a bespoke coat and a costume. Because every coat in this collection is made to your measurements in outerwear-grade fabric, it functions as a real coat. The Matrix 1 and Reloaded wool versions are ankle-length structured outerwear built to handle cold weather. Many clients wear them as statement coats to events, dinners, and photoshoots as well as for cosplay. A polyester costume cannot do this. A bespoke wool coat can.

**Q:** How do I know which measurements to take for a floor-length coat?  
**A:** The shoulder width and chest are the two most critical measurements for a structured coat — the shoulder seam placement determines how the entire coat hangs, and the chest determines whether the front lies flat or pulls. You enter all measurements on the product page, where each field has a guide. The free test coat is the safety net: if any measurement is slightly off, you catch it on the test coat before the final fabric is cut. No floor-length coat should be made without a fit check at this length.

**Q:** Is the Matrix coat made to my measurements or does it come in standard sizes?  
**A:** Every coat in this collection is made to your measurements. There are no standard sizes — no small, medium, large, or XL. You enter your measurements on the product page and the coat is cut to those numbers, including a free test coat before the final piece is made. A floor-length coat that does not fit the shoulder and chest correctly does not look the way it does on screen. Made-to-measure is the only way to get the silhouette right.

**Q:** Can I request modifications — different lining, buttons, or length?  
**A:** Yes. Lining color, button choice, pocket style, and length adjustments are all possible on most coats in this collection. Specify your customization preferences when placing your order, and the team will confirm what is achievable for the specific coat. Most clients choose a custom lining color as a personal detail while keeping the exterior as close to the original as possible.

**Q:** What is the difference between the wool Matrix Reloaded coat and the leather version?  
**A:** Both follow the same Matrix Reloaded silhouette — mandarin collar, hidden placket, below-ankle length — but in different materials. The wool version is made in monks&#8217; wool, which gives it the heavy, structured drape seen in the film and makes it functional as cold-weather outerwear. The leather version is cut from premium full-grain leather for a more fitted, elevated finish suited equally to daily wear and cosplay. Both are made to your exact measurements with the same free test coat process.

**Q:** How long does production take and where do you ship?  
**A:** Production takes approximately four to six weeks from measurement confirmation, which includes the free test coat stage. Orders ship worldwide by trackable courier. Delivery typically adds five to ten business days depending on your location. For time-sensitive orders — a convention, a shoot, or an event with a fixed date — contact us before ordering so we can confirm the timeline honestly.
