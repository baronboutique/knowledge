# Bespoke Tailoring

Bespoke tailoring is Baron Boutique's primary and defining service. Every garment is made from scratch to the individual customer's measurements. A pattern is drafted by hand; nothing is adapted from a block size.

## The Test Garment (Toile)

The distinguishing feature of the Baron Boutique bespoke process is the complimentary cotton test garment, also called a fitting garment or toile.

Before any final fabric is cut, Baron Boutique makes the garment in cotton and ships it to the customer. The customer:

1. Tries on the test garment
2. Takes clear photos (front, back, two sides)
3. Emails photos and written fit feedback to customerservice@baronboutique.com

The test garment does not need to be returned. It belongs to the customer.

Baron Boutique then adjusts the pattern based on the feedback and produces the final garment in the selected fabric. If a second fitting is needed, a second test garment may be sent.

## Timelines

| Stage | Production | Shipping | Total (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Test garment | ~21 business days | 4–9 business days | ~4 weeks |
| Final garment | ~21 business days | 4–9 business days | ~4 weeks after feedback |
| **Total** | | | **~8–10 weeks** |

All timelines are approximate and may vary with fabric availability, order complexity, and measurement confirmation.

Rush service is available on request at no additional cost, subject to production capacity.

## What Can Be Made Bespoke

All tailored garments are available as bespoke:

- Suits, blazers, jackets
- Coats, overcoats, frock coats
- Tuxedos, dinner jackets
- Dresses, skirts, trousers, shirts
- [Screen-inspired garments](/okf/services/screen-inspired.md)
- Wedding garments (men and women)

## Pattern Storage

Customer measurement patterns are stored and updated after each order and each alteration, building a personalized fit record for future orders.
