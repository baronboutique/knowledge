# Linen and Himalayan Hemp

This is probably one of the conversations we enjoy most, because the answer surprises many customers. Very few people ask us for hemp. Most ask for linen. The conversation changes after we ask one question:

"What bothers you most about linen?"

If the customer smiles and says, "Nothing, I love the relaxed wrinkles," then linen is often exactly the right fabric. The creasing is not a flaw to them; it is the look.

But if they answer, "I love natural fabrics, but I don't want to look heavily creased halfway through the day," the conversation almost always shifts toward Himalayan hemp.

## Why hemp, and why that isn't the real reason

Technically, hemp is stronger than linen. It also tends to wrinkle less aggressively while becoming softer with wear. Those are useful facts, but they aren't the reason we recommend it.

We recommend hemp because we've learned that many customers aren't actually looking for linen. They're looking for a natural fabric that stays presentable for longer. Understanding that difference changes the recommendation entirely, and it is why the wrinkle question comes before any talk of fiber strength.

Baron Boutique works with 100% natural Himalayan hemp grown at high altitude in Nepal, a fabric very few tailoring houses offer, which is exactly why the conversation usually has to start from linen: it is the fabric customers know how to ask for.
