# Dupioni Silk

Dupioni silk is one of the fabrics customers remember long after they've forgotten its technical specifications. Many choose it for its beautiful texture and the way it catches the light: the natural slubs give every garment a sense of individuality that perfectly smooth fabrics simply cannot replicate. No two lengths of Dupioni are quite alike, which suits a house where no two garments are either.

## The question that decides it

Before recommending Dupioni, we usually ask: "How often do you expect to wear this garment?"

If the answer is every week, we often guide the conversation toward wool instead (see [Understanding Wool](wool.md)), because a working wardrobe asks for a fabric that shrugs off repetition.

If the answer is a wedding, an evening event, a movie replica, or a garment that celebrates an important occasion, Dupioni immediately becomes one of our favorite options.

Our recommendation isn't based on whether Dupioni is "better." It's based on whether it matches the life the garment is going to have.

## The wider silk family

Dupioni is the signature, but the workshop also tailors satin silk, crepe silk, and other raw silks. The same objective-first logic applies: satin where the customer wants smooth formal light (lapels, linings, gowns), crepe where a dress needs fluid drape with a matte face, raw silks where texture itself is the design. The customer's occasion and expectations pick the silk; the silk never picks itself (see [How We Choose Fabric](fabric-selection.md)).
