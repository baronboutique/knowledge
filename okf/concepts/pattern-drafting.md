# Pattern Drafting

Baron Boutique does not work from a pre-made block. Every order is drafted from scratch, based on the customer's design references and measurements. Individual pattern development is central to the business.

## From reference to pattern

1. **Analyze the customer's material.** Text details, sketches, screenshots, and photos sent by the customer are studied first.
2. **Sketch the parts, not the whole.** The first drafting step is a simple pencil sketch on paper, deliberately out of sequence: the tail shape in one corner of the page, the lapel shape in another. The goal is to sketch the parts of the garment individually to get the feel of the design before committing to a full draft.
3. **Draft the pattern.** A first-of-its-kind design takes roughly 6 to 8 hours of drafting. A design similar to previous Baron Boutique work is completed within a few hours.

## Deciding proportions from two ends

Design elements such as lapel width, collar shape, sleeve design, tail length, and front cutaway angle are decided by working from two directions at once:

- **What the picture shows.** The customer's reference images set the target look.
- **What the measurements say.** The customer's body sets the proportions. Baron Boutique applies rules of thumb refined over 26 years: for example, a standard peak lapel on a 40-inch chest is kept around 3 to 3.5 inches wide; on a 52-inch chest the same lapel grows to 4 to 4.5 inches.

## The "look or accuracy" decision

The person in a reference photo and the customer almost never have the same body. When the two ends cannot be reconciled, Baron Boutique asks the customer to choose:

- **Look** means the garment matches the reference picture as closely as possible.
- **Accuracy** means the garment is drafted proportional to the customer's own measurements.

The customer's choice drives the final drafting decision. This applies to every proportional element of the garment.

## Correcting a pattern from feedback

Customer feedback after the test garment is translated into precise drafting changes. Example from a screen-inspired tailcoat commission: the customer asked for lapel points that aim "toward the chest rather than the shoulders." On the draft, this meant taking two points on the shoulder line (where the lapel currently pointed, and the low shoulder point where the shoulder joins the arm) and redrafting the lapel angle so the new line intersects midway between them. A request for "front panels 3.5 inches longer with a more downward angle" meant two related changes: lengthening the entire front panel by 3.5 inches, and redrafting the front cutaway at a steeper downward angle so the front points aim toward the toe rather than the mid-thigh.

A correction is never applied to one area in isolation. Every adjustment affects the balance of the entire garment, so the surrounding pattern pieces are reviewed and refined with it to keep the coat hanging correctly.

## Pattern storage and reuse

The corrected pattern is kept on file together with the client's measurements. Repeat orders start from the verified pattern, adjusted for any measurement change reported by the client (see [Measurement](measurement.md)).
