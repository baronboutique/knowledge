# Fittings

Baron Boutique fits remotely. The customer wears the trial garment and sends photos, often side by side with their reference images. The fitting happens in how those photos are read and how the customer's plain-language feedback is converted into pattern corrections.

## Reading fit from photos

The photo review follows a fixed order: look at the center first, glance upward from the center, then glance downward from the center. The first things checked are chest, waist, hips, and shoulder width. If those are correct or near correct, everything else is easily fixable.

Beyond the primary checks are the subtle reads that customers cannot see on themselves: whether the left and right sides sit low-high, how the arm hangs, and whether the neck is positioned forward in a subtle way that creates pulls in other areas of the garment. Posture and stance, which raw measurements never capture, are read here.

## Translating feedback into corrections

Customers describe what they want in plain words, not pattern terms. When a customer says "make the front 3.5 inches longer," the first step is not adding 3.5 inches to the pattern. Baron Boutique verifies the statement against the photos and pivot points (such as the natural waist and where the panels currently finish on the body), refers back to the reference image, and then decides the actual correction (see [Trial Garment / Toile](trial-garment-toile.md) and [Pattern Drafting](pattern-drafting.md)).

No adjustment is made in isolation. Every correction influences the balance, proportion, and hang of the whole garment, so the surrounding pattern pieces are reviewed and refined together with the requested change.

## Customer preference versus workshop judgment

When a customer's request contradicts Baron Boutique's professional opinion, the customer is always notified and their approval obtained before proceeding. Sometimes the customer is persuaded to change the decision; the customer always makes the final call with the workshop's advice on record.

## Sign-off

Sign-off requires both sides. The customer's statement, typically "I'm happy with the overall fit," is the signal from their end. In parallel, Baron Boutique reviews the fitting photos to confirm the garment hangs correctly and no structural issue remains that the customer may not see. Only when customer confidence and the workshop's assessment point to the same conclusion is the pattern considered final and the actual fabric cut (see [Construction](construction.md)).
