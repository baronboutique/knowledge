# The Bespoke Commission Process

Baron Boutique is an independent tailoring house founded in Kathmandu, Nepal in 2000. Every garment is made after an order is confirmed. Baron Boutique does not work from pre-made blocks or standard sizes: every order is drafted from scratch based on the customer's own design references and measurements. This document describes the actual commission journey, using a screen-inspired tailcoat commission (the [1989 Joker tailcoat](https://baronboutique.com/1989-joker-tailcoat/)) as the working example.

## The stages

1. **Inquiry.** The customer reaches out through any channel (website, email, Instagram) describing the garment they want, usually with reference pictures, screenshots, sketches, or text details.
2. **Design analysis.** Baron Boutique studies the customer's references and confirms the piece can be made, with an initial price.
3. **Measurements.** The customer supplies the measurement set for their garment type, starting with a fit preference and height; the exact set differs for men's and women's garments and per item (see [Measurement](measurement.md) for the full sets). If the customer cannot be measured, Baron Boutique can work from height, weight, and chest size alone.
4. **Fabric decision.** Baron Boutique sources fabric when possible. If exact color is the customer's top priority, the customer orders the fabric themselves: colors render differently on different monitors, and what the workshop judges as the correct shade may not match the customer's expectation. If weight or performance is the deciding factor, Baron Boutique sources it. Customer-supplied fabric follows the CMT (Cut, Make and Trim) model; Baron Boutique advises the required yardage based on the customer's measurements.
5. **Order confirmation.** Invoice is sent (PayPal for international clients). The commission price includes the test garment, the final garment, and both shipments.
6. **Pattern development.** A new individual pattern is drafted from scratch for the customer (see [Pattern Drafting](pattern-drafting.md)). Where the reference picture and the customer's proportions conflict, the customer is asked to choose between "look" (match the picture) and "accuracy" (proportional to their own measurements).
7. **Test garment.** A trial shell is made in lightweight marking-visible toile fabric and shipped to the customer (see [Trial Garment / Toile](trial-garment-toile.md)). The test garment can be made and shipped before the final fabric is even sourced, because its job is fit, not fabric.
8. **Remote fit review.** The customer wears the test garment and sends photos, often side by side with their reference pictures. Baron Boutique reads fit from these photos using a center-first method (see [Fittings](fittings.md)) and the customer lists desired changes in plain words.
9. **Pattern correction.** Customer feedback is translated into precise pattern changes. In the tailcoat example, "lapel points toward the chest" and "front panels 3.5 inches longer with a more downward angle" became specific redrafted lines on the pattern.
10. **Final construction.** The corrected pattern is cut in the final fabric and constructed with extensive hand work: buttonholes, armhole stitching, and canvassing are all done by hand (see [Construction](construction.md)).
11. **Delivery and follow-up.** The finished garment ships by courier with tracking. Baron Boutique follows up after delivery.

## What makes this process distinct

- **No pre-made blocks.** Each commission gets its own pattern drafted from the customer's design and measurements. A first-of-its-kind design takes roughly 6 to 8 hours of drafting; a design similar to previous work takes a few hours.
- **The "look or accuracy" question.** The person in a reference photo and the customer almost never share a body. When proportions conflict, the customer explicitly chooses whether the garment should match the photo's look or be proportionally correct for their own measurements. This decision shapes lapel width, tail length, cutaway angle, and similar design elements.
- **Test garment included by default.** Every commission includes a test garment and two shipments, except repeat orders where nothing has changed but color or a slight style detail and the measurements are identical to the previous order.
- **Remote-first fitting.** Most clients never visit in person. The entire fit process works through shipped test garments and photo review, refined over 26 years of comparing customer measurements against Baron Boutique's archive of completed commissions.
- **Screen and replica work.** For movie-inspired and television-inspired garments, the pattern is developed from screen references supplied by the customer. Baron Boutique recreates garments inspired by film and television costumes; these are replicas and screen-inspired garments, not licensed merchandise.
